The island had been the site of Project Black Sun Rising, a classified experiment attempting to weaponize the fusion of magitek technology with void energy and living tissue. Now the isle was surrounded by unnatural storms that moved against the wind, causing any approaching vessel to simply disappear, while the facility itself had become a living nightmare where walls shifted overnight and corridors materialized from nothing.

During the meeting, Varinius revealed the true horror of their failed experiments when he exposed his right arm—blackened flesh marbled with pulsing violet veins that moved with their own rhythm, ending in jagged, overgrown nails. This corruption was the result of Project Black Sun Rising's "success" in binding void energy to living tissue, and it was slowly spreading through his body. He desperately needed Livia because she had developed the only known stabilizing serum using her own blood as the base, and all research notes had been destroyed when the facility fell. The corruption posed not just a personal threat to Varinius, but potentially a catastrophic danger if it spread to the mainland, making the mission as much about preventing a larger disaster as rescuing one person.

Despite the astronomical mortality rate and obvious dangers, the Phoenix Vanguard accepted the contract after Varinius offered 300,000 gil upfront per person, doubled upon successful retrieval. The group would have access to the Scylla, a modified reconnaissance vessel equipped with void-baffling alloys and purified ceruleum engines designed to pierce Stormveil's supernatural defenses. Delika, revealed as the only person to have entered Stormveil and survived, would serve as their guide. 

Though some members expressed serious reservations and Orlando's sensitivity to corrupted aether made him physically ill around Varinius, the team ultimately committed to the expedition. They prepared to depart immediately, understanding that time was running out and that the living island itself might prove to be their greatest enemy.
