The Vengeful Phoenix rocked gently at port, her crew gathered on deck beneath a darkening sky. Storm clouds piled on the horizon, black and heavy with rain.

Xyla walked to her usual spot at the rail and leaned against it, her violet eyes settling on Z'iro with a frown. "Have you told them yet?" She raised a brow.

We're going to be going a bit outside of our guidelines on this one, the very rare circumstance that we do. There's a Maelstrom treasure galleon out at sea not far from here, it's callin' for storms so under the cover of said storm, we're going to go take all they got. Supposedly, about five million gil pieces all in individual pretty golden coins."

The crew stirred. Z'iro let the number settle before continuing.

"Obviously, no small payout, so we should do pretty well if this goes well enough, but they won't be helpless either, given their cargo, so we'll be in for a fight. No survivors this time around, aye? No one gets back to their admiral to spin tales of us robbin' them blind. If it makes you feel any better, these will all be very veteran marines and officers."

Rove looked around at the others and let out a nervous chuckle. "That's just grand. I'll be sure to make myself useful by being faaaaar below deck..." He started toward the stairs.

Orlando let a small grin slip onto his face, pulling out a cigar and lighting it. "This sounds like it will be a good time."

Cassian gave a singular nod, his hand drifting toward the hilt of his weapon. "Understood. Dead men don't file reports or give chase." His voice dropped to a cold, professional tone. "If they're veterans, they know the stakes as well as we do."

Fiora rolled her chair after Rove, pulling a small flask from a concealed pocket at her side. She refused to make eye contact with V'ika. V'ika closed his journal. Without a word, he followed Fiora below deck.

Xyla pushed off from the rail, her expression deadly serious. "If any of you ruin our reputation on land or sea because you had a soft heart moment, you are going to regret it because I will put you in the phoenix and listen to your screams." She paused, eyeing the crew. "Understood? Also, no names and don't be leaving anything behind either."

Skyas shrugged. "Well then, I can only hope that the people in the Maelstrom that I like are at home and that it's mostly the nepo babies on board."

Cassian nodded once more. "Understood." His eyes followed Fiora until she disappeared from view, then he focused on Xyla and Z'iro again.

The Dream Hare stood apart from the others, gazing up at the sky. Something about the gathering clouds left them unsettled.

"Though that begs to question Captain, will we burn the boat after collecting our spoils?" Orlando asked, cigar smoke curling from his lips.

Z’iro replies, "Everything we can take, comes back to the ship. They go to the bottom, we sail off into the sunset Orlando."

Ayre blinked. Blinked again. "...Uhhh... wait what?! We are hunting down the Maelstrom?"

Anya's voice came quiet. "Five million gil buys a lot of silence. Storm cover, veteran marines... no survivors." Her fingers brushed her deck once, then stopped. "I won't dress this up as something it isn't. But if we're committing, then half-measures will only spill our blood instead of theirs."

Cassian glanced at the sky, his hand still resting on his weapon. "When do we depart?"

As he made his way across the deck, Rove continued his protests to no one in particular. "I truly don't know why I keep being put in such dangerous situations. I mean honestly, what are they thinking putting a merchant so close to danger! I'd only get in the way!"

Fiora raised an eyebrow at him, taking a long draw from her flask. "Do you want the gil or not?"

V'ika came down the stairs behind them. "What's the flask for?"

"Hydration, what else?" She deadpanned.

From above, Skyas shouted down with a smirk, "You are the company you keep, and you chose adrenaline junky mercenaries and scoundrels."

Z'iro grunted and turned back to the wheel. "We depart now, so anyone without the stomach for this is free to depart within two minutes, otherwise start preparing yourselves. Once we hit the storm I expect you all to be on deck keeping things in order, if you aren't needed on deck, strap in anything that might start rollin' around below." He glanced back over his shoulder. "Ugan, on the rigging, once we hit the storm I want us reefed to half sail."

Cassian pushed off the railing. "I'm heading below to strap down the crates and supplies then."

V'ika looked at him. "Take Fiora with you. She's been reckless while you've been gone. Please." A hint of worry crept into his voice.

Cassian looked at V'ika, then shifted his gaze to Fiora. "Stay close." His words came gentle but firm.

Xyla smirked, pushing herself off the rail. "Well, you know where I'll be." She adjusted her bow on her back and headed for the rigging, climbing toward the tallest crow's nest. "Remember what I said, Captain." She shot Z'iro a glare as she went.

The storm waited on the horizon. The Vengeful Phoenix began to move.


## Leaving Port

The Vengeful Phoenix left port under cover of darkness and rain, sliding out among the shadows like a ghost ship. All lights snuffed until they were far from shore.

The storm found them quickly.

Howling wind drove rain sideways across the deck. The sea roiled and churned, lightning forking across blackened clouds. Despite her size, the Phoenix rose high on each swell and dropped hard, slicing through waves toward the treasure galleon. Thunder rolled overhead, swallowing every other sound.

Z'iro yelled over the chaos from the wheel. "First Mate Stormgaze, on deck! Ugan, reef back to half and prepare for quarter once we close on the Gilded Rose." The enemy's name, spoken for the first time. "Vylerni, get the deck crew in order, boarding hooks and lines ready. Cassian, roll out second deck cannons when we start to close, grapeshot for their deck, chain for their masts, keep the rest stowed on lower decks, we don't mean to sink them before we can take what they have."

He fought the wheel as he spoke, muscles straining against the storm's pull.

Ugan made his way carefully across the high mast, wary of the slick beam and the wind that threatened to tear him loose. "Aye, Aye!" His voice nearly drowned in the storm's fury.

Just below the main main deck, Rove had finally calmed after the better part of an hour rambling to himself. He sipped something from a bottle, staying well clear of the busy crew.

"Aye Captain!" Skyas shouted back, stepping down to help keep supplies and cannons from rolling overboard as the ship pitched.

Cassian snapped a sharp reply. "Understood, Captain." He turned on his heel, pushing Fiora's chair to a corner further below deck. "Rove, V'ika, with me. Secure the heavy shot and prep the hatch. I want those doors ready to open on Z'iro's mark."

Xyla heard the order barked at her—work usually left to others—and rolled her eyes. She kicked open a crate stowed in her crow's nest, attached her three pouches of usual concoctions, and wrapped cloth around her hand before taking a rope. She swung down, sliding the length effortlessly, landing almost soundless on the deck.

She began checking over hooks and lines, expertly ordering lost-looking crew into place. Her anger rose when one of the newer members stood staring at the hustle.

"Don't just stand there or you can go down with 'em, move it, get those hooks ready to go now—I don't have time to be holding your damn hand."

"WHAT?!" Rove's voice cut through from below. "What do you mean with you!? I wouldn't be any help doing the cannons! They're too heavy!" Despite his protests, he followed Cassian.

V'ika said nothing as he descended to aid with the guns.

On deck, Anya gripped the rail. "This is insane..." She had survived Garlean occupation, healed broken resistance fighters in bombed-out buildings while airships screamed overhead. She'd never been in a naval battle during a storm.


---

Muttered profanities echoed from where Cassian had left Fiora. Her chair slammed into a crate as the ship pitched. She wrenched the wheels, trying to brace against the deck's angle. The Phoenix dropped hard. Her stomach lurched. The chair rolled backward, picking up speed.

Her flask fell somewhere. She could hear it rolling, metal scraping wood with each swell.

V'ika appeared moments later and grabbed her wheelchair. "Oh hush, you're the one who wheeled yourself here!" He dragged her toward the crates, fighting to keep them all in one spot.

Fiora twisted to glare at him, knuckles white on the armrests as the ship lurched again. She didn't fight him. Her hands found purchase against the nearest crate, fingers digging into the wood grain.


---

On the upper deck, Orlando watched the organized chaos before making his way to Skyas. "May I assist you?"

A loose barrel went bouncing across the deck, narrowly missing him.

Skyas slammed his foot down on a wheel that had come loose, tying it back while using his leg to hold it steady. Already drenched. "Go for it, tie down anything that seems loose." He watched the barrel whir past. "Kinda like that."

Orlando nodded and went to secure the wayward cargo.

The Vengeful Phoenix charged through the storm, smashing through the waves.

From the rigging, Ugan worked a knot with his legs wrapped around the ropes, singing into the wind: "To Sirensong Sea we're steering straight! Way hey, blow the sirens down! Where the fog rolls thick and the lasses wait! Way hey, blo—SHIP HO, CAPTAIN!" He pointed forward. "I SPY THE MARK!"

At the cannon window, Ayre bit down on rope as she secured a cannon trying to escape its moorings. She looked out at the chaos of the storm. "By the Dusk Mother... what a fucking tempest..."

Z'iro leaned right, looking past the masts as they crested a high wave. The ship was there, just as Ugan said. "Aye, there she is! She's got her back to us—keep us at half, we're going to rake her deck with grapeshot before we come about for boarding." The Phoenix closed fast. "Ugan, get up top and fly the bloody, give them their honorable warning they'll be given no quarter."

Below, Cassian barked orders. "V'ika, tie Fiora's chair to the deck bolts, double-knot it. I want her secured before the ship starts pitching harder." He didn't wait for a reply, turning toward the guns. "Rove, get those ports cleared and the cannons prepped with ammo. Stand by for the Captain's mark."

V'ika found rope and tied Fiora's chair to the deck bolts, double-knotted as ordered. Then he ran to help prep the cannons, moving boxes so Rove would have an easier time.

"I swear you all forget I'm a merchant and not some strong person! The cannon balls probably weigh as much as I do!" Rove struggled with the ports, managed to move boxes of ammunition into position, and loaded at least one shot into each gun while grumbling that someone more qualified should be doing this.

On deck, Skyas sang while helping the crew, "I thought I heard the old man say: 'Leave her, Johnny, leave her.' Tomorrow ye will get yer pay, and it's time for us to leave her. Leave her, Johnny, leave her! Ooooh leave her, Johnny, leave her! For the voyage is long and the winds don't blow, and it's time for us to leave her."

Ugan carried out Z'iro's order with a devilish grin, switching out the colors—the bloody flag rising—singing unintelligibly through the storm.

Orlando finished tying another barrel and looked toward where Ugan had pointed. The storm put out his cigar. "Finally the fun part is near." A manic smile formed on his face.

"Cassian, load the cannons, roll them out!" Z'iro called down to Skyas. "Skyas, with the boarding crew, hooks across, you're with the first landing party, clear the rest of the crew a path to cross. Take Oleander and Ayre with you!"

Xyla watched the hooks laid out and ready, some to be thrown, some shot across. A crew member on each. One of the newer sailors threw up over the side.

"Pull yourself together or you're going in." She glared at him, then looked back toward her nest in the rigging. She decided against returning. Instead, she pulled her bow from her back and took the highest spot she could on the top deck.

As they closed on the Gilded Rose, Anya felt a pang of sympathy for the Maelstrom crew. They'd noticed far too late what was upon them. All of them were going to die.

She drew a card.

"Just heal... just a healer... nothing more..." She spoke to herself more than anyone nearby. "That's all I can afford to be."


## Preparing for a Fight

The Gilded Rose spotted them approaching off her stern. Three cannon rounds burst from her left side—a warning shot.

Z'iro laughed. He kept the Phoenix pointed forward.

"She's baring her teeth at us, she means to fight! Cassian, prep the crews, hold your fire until I give the command—aim for their top deck, give the boarding crew room to breathe!"

The Gilded Rose began rolling out cannons on both her gun decks. Two full rows.

Cassian's voice cut through the storm. "Understood, Captain!" He turned toward the guns. "Hold for the mark! Aim for the top deck, V'ika. Keep the ports ready to open, Rove! No one fires until the Captain gives the word!" He glanced at Fiora, just long enough to confirm she was secure, then turned back to his task.

On deck, Ayre drew her axe. Fire aether blazed crimson from haft to head as she planted one foot on the railing, waiting for the order to board. "Karash... you may get your wish and drink much blood tonight..."

Ugan held fast to the rigging, shouting with glee. "We'll spear through the codfish and feed 'em to the sharks! Gahaha! That booty'll be ours, it will!"

Below, Rove shifted nervously near the cannons. "This is gonna get really loud isn't it... I should have you know I have sensitive ears and could very well miss a vital order if I'm kept near a loud exploding cannon, perhaps I'd be better served elsewhere?"

Cassian gave him a dry smile. "Good try, merchant. This'll be some good experience for you."

The gap between the ships closed.

Z'iro gave his final commands. "Ugan, reef to quarter now, when we come about, bring us close!"

Across the water, the crew of the Gilded Rose came into view. Veteran marines, every one of them. Ready. Not a single nervous face among them as they raised their weapons.

Z'iro's voice rang out. "Now! Fire now!"

V'ika nodded and moved to his position. He looked at Rove. "You having hearing damage!? You're not the one with animalistic ears here! Now stop complaining!"

The Dream Hare placed their hands above their head, dampening the sound around them before it began.

Cassian's arm dropped like an axe, signaling Rove to open the ports. "Fire!" The word echoed through the second deck. Ports flew open. Cassian fired gun after gun—all preloaded, all aimed. He stood firm against the recoil. "Rove, V'ika, reload! Keep that fire going!"

Screams cut through the storm. High. Desperate. Human.

On deck, Anya's hands trembled. The card she'd been holding fluttered away into the wind. "They're people," she whispered, but her voice sounded distant even to her own ears. Her planisphere felt heavier. The metal warmer. She could feel it now—the other shape it could take. The weight it wanted to be.

Xyla nocked her bow, fingers wrapping around the string. She drew back and a black arrow appeared, jet steel with blue sparks skittering down the shaft. She aimed for the first crewman on the opposite ship. Waited for the prime moment.

V'ika grabbed ammunition and helped load the cannon, covering his ears each time it fired. His tail twitched with every blast. Load, cover, fire. Load, cover, fire.

The Dream Hare moved to the rear of the ship, walking calmly before drawing their staff and lifting from the deck.

Ugan scrambled across the rigging, working the sails with expert hands despite the slick rope and howling wind. He nearly lost his footing twice but recovered each time. "Kami, protect me from this accursed storm!"

Skyas helped the crew keep adequate ammunition flowing to the guns until boarding time.

The first volley's echo left Rove stumbling. He squinted as Cassian shouted something—a Rive? He shook his head, ears ringing, and worked the cannons opposite V'ika, trying to keep a consistent line ready to fire.


---

Below, in the darkness between the crates, Fiora waited until the footsteps faded and the others left. Her hands found the armrests. She shifted her weight forward and dropped to her knees on the planks. She crawled toward the sound of metal scraping wood, following the flask's roll. Found it wedged against a beam. Her fingers closed around it.

She sat back against the beam and unscrewed the cap.


## Boarding

Grapeshot ripped across the deck of the Gilded Rose as the Phoenix's second deck cannons roared through the storm. The Rose returned fire, full cannonballs that punched into the Phoenix's third deck hull. Above the waterline. The armored internal plating held. Minimal damage.

The crew of the Gilded Rose stumbled back from the deck's edge where the grapeshot had torn through.

Z'iro swung the Phoenix around and brought her alongside the Rose. "Hooks across now, second deck crew leave the cannons, secure the lines—sails to close, boarding crews across—take the ship, no quarter!"

He released the wheel and made his way down to the deck, scanning the Rose for their Captain.

Orlando flicked his cigar aside. His hand plunged through a tear and pulled out the Sanguine Harvester—dangling chains and gemstones letting out a keening sound. He looked to Ayre and Skyas. "Shall we make it a competition or would that be too cruel?"

The teal glow in Ayre's eye faded. Her red eye began to glow instead. She stared out at the ship. "...Not exactly the best time to be trying out that training D'vaari told me... Hells..."

Hooks flew across and latched onto their target. Skyas grew impatient waiting for everything to fasten. He spotted a rope from the mast in a convenient position. "I've always wanted to try this out..." He climbed halfway up the middle mast, grabbed the rope, and cut another nearby. The chain reaction sent him swinging across to the enemy ship. "Move it or lose it Orlando and Ayre, you heard the captain! Let's kick the party off with a bang!"

He swung onto the Rose's deck and drop-kicked a marine with incredible force as a landing. "And finally, the party begins!"

His blade came free. He dashed into the crowd of marines, slashing through several as he weaved through attacks. After a good few kills, he jumped back toward where Orlando and Ayre would land—making sure they wouldn't get overrun before the rest of the Vanguard could join.

"Slake my thirst." Orlando commanded the surprised marines. He dashed forward and slashed into the nearest one, blood splattering as he violently ripped the blade free. He stepped through a tear and appeared amidst the closest group. Arms burst through another tear and started ripping into people.

From the Phoenix, Xyla aimed for the first target she could see on the other ship. The arrow flew true—landed in the head. He dropped to the floor with a loud thunk.

Ayre swung behind Skyas, axe still out. She landed on top of a marine, her axe cleaving from clavicle to hip before she kicked the corpse away. Her gaze filled with fire as she charged ahead, cutting through bodies and steel like nothing at all, leaping from fight to fight.

"RAAAAAH!"

Once the boarding crew cleared a path, Z'iro stepped up onto the edge of the Phoenix's deck. "Everyone across, now!"

He grabbed a rope and swung across, joining the boarding party.


---

Anya should move. Should follow. Should position herself to heal the wounded, shield the vulnerable. She stumbled toward the boarding planks, but her legs felt wrong. Too heavy. The planisphere in her hands was burning now, the metal searing her palms. She couldn't let go.

"Good," Vesper purred. "Finally."

Her hands moved without her permission. The planisphere twisted in her grip, celestial metal flowing like water, reshaping. The familiar weight of her star globe split, elongated—the crystal core fracturing into twin pommels as curved steel bloomed. Poor Anya. Screaming in the back of their shared skull, beating uselessly against the mental barriers Vesper had just torn through like wet parchment.

"Gods, I missed this."


---

Ugan wastes no time in clambering across the rigging and swinging on a loose line, practically flinging himself onto the enemy ship. He pulls his spear into one hand as he lands, his eyes darting across the enemy deck seeking his prey.

Xyla took a rope and ran, swinging across to the Rose. She headed straight for their rigging and climbed into a nest, planting an arrow into its occupant. He fell backward onto the deck with a loud crash.

Below, Cassian heard Z'iro's order. He scooped Fiora up in a secure grip. "Hang on," he muttered against her ear.

He looked back at V'ika. "V'ika, bring her chair. Throw it to me once I'm on the other side!"

With Fiora held firmly against his chest, Cassian timed the movement of the sea and leaped across the gap. He landed hard on the galleon's wood and immediately sought cover, his body shielding hers as he drew his axe.

V’ika picked up the wheelchair and chucked it across to Cassian. He didn't bother with the rope. He used his globe to cast a spell and leaped across to the ship.

The wheelchair hit the deck beside them with a clatter. Cassian set Fiora down and she caught the armrests on instinct, steadying herself. Someone screamed. Focus. She needed to focus. She tracked the crew's movement, drawing aether from Dawnbringer in preparation.

Rove didn't know why, but he followed Cassian and the others. Straight into the danger.

Cassian stood between Rove and Fiora. He glanced at Rove briefly. "Get ready, merchant. If you have weapons, you best get them out of their sheathe now. We'll get some blood on your hands before we're out of here."


## The Forces Meet

The grappling hooks bit into the Gilded Rose's rail, and the pirates of the Vengeful Phoenix swarmed across the gap between the two vessels.

Z'iro led the boarding party, his boots hitting the deck as the first wave of his crew rushed past, engaging the Maelstrom marines in combat that turned vicious within seconds. Steel met steel across the rain-slicked planks. Veterans on both sides, and neither willing to yield an ilm. Blood mixed with seawater and ran between the boards.

Z'iro turned toward the helm. "On me. We end this quickly."

He moved up the stairs, the rest of the group following close behind. At the top, Senior Captain Alexstrom and First Mate Symas turned to face them, weapons already in hand. The captain's deep-set features twisted into a scowl beneath his officer's cap as he spoke, "Pirate filth. The admiral will hear of this."

Z'iro strolled to the side, drawing his twin blades. "Afraid she won't, friend. This is an awful vicious gale. Ships go down all the time." He settled into a ready stance, buying time for the others to join him. "I'm sure you'll be remembered fondly. As a man of principle who finally lost his nerve under the pressure."

Behind him, the boarding party assembled.

"I ALREADY TOLD YOU ALL I DON'T HAVE WEAPONS!" Rove shouted at Cassian, louder than the situation warranted given their surroundings. The Endless Journey hummed at his hip, then offered him something unexpected—a potato gun. Stranger still, the potato loaded into its barrel had a grenade pin lodged in it.

Cassian smirked. "That looks like a gun to me."

"What in the—oh gods..." Rove held it up, swinging the barrel wildly toward anyone he didn't recognize. "DON'T MAKE ME DO IT. I DON'T KNOW WHAT IT'LL DO BUT IT'LL DO SOMETHIN' BAD, I'M SURE!"

Ayre had no words. Steam poured from her mouth with each exhale, breath hot against the storm wind. Her grip on her axe tightened until her knuckles went white, and the weapon ignited—flames completely engulfing the blade, casting dancing shadows across her face. A feral beast unchained.

Ugan raised his spear with a laugh that boomed over the clash of combat below. "The sea will shine crimson tomorrow! Gahahaha! Come on, Onion Merchant, let's peel these bastards to bones!"

Xyla pulled back, two glittering jet-black arrows materializing in her bow as she stepped to one side and took aim at the First Mate.

Skyas was mid-clash with one of the enemy crew when his ears flicked toward the helm. He kicked the nameless marine away and gathered enough aether in his legs to leap up and join the others. Upon closer inspection of First Mate Symas, he was taken aback. The man's stance, his bearing, "It's like a lawful good me..."

Orlando cleaved through another marine, cackling as he watched the others group up. "That he does, Skyas!"

Something had shifted in Anya's body. The way she held herself, the set of her shoulders, the curl of her smile. Whatever gentle healer had been there moments before was gone. In the back of their shared mind, Anya sobbed: Please, please, they'll see, they'll know, please just let me back—

"Not a chance in the seven hells, princess," Vesper laughed aloud. She looked up at the Maelstrom Captain and let her twin katanas catch the lightning. "Thanks for the dance, darling."

Chanting to themselves, the Dream Hare murmured, barely audible beneath the storm, “Something benevolent this way comes…”

Cassian pushed Fiora's chair through the chaos of the melee, setting her down carefully behind the main lines. "Stay low," he said, his voice steady despite the carnage surrounding them. "If you need me, call for me. I'll hear you." She gave a sharp nod. He pivoted and cut a direct path through the fighting toward the enemy captain.

Fiora's hands moved through the motions, pulling aether from Dawnbringer. As she wove, a shield gathered around Z'iro, invisible but solid. The glow faded from her fingers as she finished. She reached for the flask at her hip, tipped it back. Sweat beaded at her hairline, tracking down her temple. Her skin had gone gray in the storm's light.

V'ika drew a card from his deck and glanced at it before throwing it toward Ayre. The shield it carried settled over her like a second skin.


---

Senior Captain Alexstrom and First Mate Symas moved back to back, then seized the initiative. They charged forward as one, coordinated despite the chaos, but remained close to each other.

The Captain stormed toward Z'iro. Their blades locked, and the two exchanged rapid, precise blows—steel meeting steel in loud crashes. Both wielded dual swords. Every time Alexstrom's blade met Z'iro's, the Bloodroses sparked red until Z'iro gained the upper hand. A vicious slash opened the Captain's chest, followed by a brutal right hook that sent the man stumbling backward with a growl of pain. Alexstrom turned, seeking a new opponent, and locked blades with Ayre.

First Mate Symas charged Skyas, arcing his blade in from the right to catch him off guard.

Skyas swung his katana around and met the attack with a clean parry. The two clashed for several heartbeats, sparks flying with each impact, before Skyas pushed him back. With one final parry, he lifted his boot and slammed it into Symas's stomach.

"If you're looking to land a blow," Skyas said as the man stumbled away, "you're gonna have to try a little harder than that."

Symas retreated, his pride wounded more than his body. He looked around, then moved to engage Orlando instead.

Ayre charged forward to meet the Captain's blade. Her axe clashed against his sword before she pushed him off with a hard kick to his sternum. "COME ON!" Her voice was low, filled with rage. "DON'T TELL ME THE CAPTAIN PUNCHED YOU SO HARD YER STILL DIZZY FROM IT!"

As the Captain's blade rose toward Ayre, Fiora's hands moved before the thought could form, aether weaving into a thick shield around Ayre just before steel came down to bear. She drew the remaining energy toward herself, one hand pressing against her side.

Rove still clutched the potato-gun-grenade. Too many allies nearby for whatever it would do. Instead, he reached into his bag and hurled a pair of Glacial Boots toward the enemy.

Orlando dragged back the Sanguine Harvester and laughed as he slashed the blade across his own chest. "I finally get to learn how this works!" The wound began to glow a dull purplish-red.

Xyla let loose both her arrows. One landed in Symas's shoulder, the other in his arm. She pulled back immediately, another shimmering jet-black arrow appearing from thin air.

Cassian moved ahead with a quick jump, bringing his axe down in a hard slash against Symas before retreating back toward the backline. His position put him between the officers and those who couldn't defend themselves.

Ugan watched the melee unfold, ducking and weaving, waiting for his moment. When the enemy Captain was occupied with Ayre, he thrust his spear forward, slashing at the man's side, then leapt back out of the fray.

V'ika grabbed another card and poured the heavens into it before throwing it at the Captain. A small fire explosion bloomed on impact, and the card returned to his hand.

Z'iro followed after Symas, the Bloodroses surging with power. Bright red sparks cascaded down their lengths, connecting with the Soulblossom and empowering it as well. He slipped under a high arcing swipe aimed at his throat, parried the strike aside, and unleashed a flurry of vicious slashes that bit deep into the First Mate. On the final strike, the power within the Bloodroses exploded, releasing a bright red aura that washed across the party.

"Reveal thy soul to me." Orlando's cackle cut through the rain as he slashed into Symas with the charged Harvester, marking the man's soul.

The Dream Hare made no sound—only a strange whisper that carried for a single chime, looking toward Orlando as a watery aura enveloped him.

Vesper's right katana came in high. A feint. When Symas brought his weapon up to parry, it left his ribs exposed. Her left blade carved across them—not as deep as she wanted, but the blood ran free. She just laughed, wild and unrestrained, her blades dripping crimson as lightning split the sky overhead. "Come on then, darling," she purred. "Show me what that fancy rank bought you."

Skyas grinned, feeling the Soulblossom's aether surge alongside the Bloodroses. "This is gonna be fun!"

He placed the katana back in its sheath for a quickdraw, dashed forward, and swung the blade across Symas's chest. Blue lightning crackled along the steel.

"Divine Departure!"


## Promise of Blood

Captain Alexstrom disengaged from Ayre long enough to draw his pistols. Two shots cracked through the storm toward Xyla. He holstered the guns, drew his blades again, and re-engaged Ayre.

First Mate Symas found his opening. Two strikes connected with Orlando, knocking him back several steps. The marine's smirk cut through the downpour. "That's all you got, pirate?" He pressed forward.

"Satiate our hunger or die at my feet," Orlando replied to Symas, blade rising to cut through the cocky marine.

Xyla screamed. The arrow she'd pre-nocked dissipated in her grip as she grabbed a nearby rope and swung toward a platform closer to the fighting. Blood ran down her left thigh. More darkened her right side, at least two of the Captain's shots had found their mark. She grimaced, already pulling back her bow as she shouted, "What the hell are you all doing? Get them down!"

Rove rifled through his bag and pulled out an odd-looking pair of dice. "Just... be kind..." he grumbled, tossing them toward the Captain. The dice clattered across the wet deck. Snake eyes. Rove faceplanted into the boards.

Xyla loosed her arrows at the same moment. The arrows flew. Sailed true for a heartbeat. Then twisted into pasta and landed on the deck with a puff of dispersing green powder. The Endless Journey vibrated against Rove's back. Laughing.

"WHAT. THE. FUCK," She glared across the ship, searching for the source.

Skyas watched the spaghetti hit the deck and laughed.

Fiora felt the rush of air as bullets tore past—close enough that instinct jerked her head to the side. She tracked their path across the deck toward Xyla. Her hands moved before conscious thought, pulling aether from Dawnbringer and throwing it across the distance. The healing took hold, knitting flesh, stanching blood. Gray crept in at the edges of her vision. Her grip on the armrests went white-knuckled.

Ayre brought her axe down hard onto the Captain's blade, breaking his guard. She took the opening—a gut shot, her burning axe dragging across his stomach. She moved with the blow, forcing him to turn, putting his back to the crew behind her. "WEAK! YOU'RE NOTHING BUT A WEAKLING!" The flames on her weapon roared higher. "WHERE IS THE MIGHT OF THE MAELSTROM I HEARD SO MUCH ABOUT?"

Vesper's head snapped toward Ayre's voice. Something feral lit up in her chest. "Oh." A breath. "Oh, I like her." She circled to flank the Captain from the opposite side, her blades tracing lazy patterns through the rain. Crimson drops flew from the steel with each movement. "She's right, you know!" Vesper's voice rose with savage glee. "All that fancy decoration on your uniform, and you're bleeding out like a gutted fish!"

Cassian jumped forward with a brutal strike, parrying Symas's guard aside and burying his axe deep into the First Mate's shoulder. He didn't linger, back to the lines before the man could retaliate.

Ugan skulked around the edges of the fight until he found the Captain's blind spot. He rushed forward, jabbed his crude spear into the Captain's side, and ripped it free before scurrying back. "I'll bleed you like a stuck pig!"

V'ika poured aether into his star globe. It glowed bright orange as healing energy flowed toward Orlando, small orbs drifting from the globe toward the rest of the party.

Soulblossom still glowed with its ghostly visage, blue lightning crackling along its edge. Skyas grinned and lifted the blade overhead—an extremely telegraphed strike, practically begging Symas to parry.

The First Mate took the bait.

As the blade came down like a bolt of lightning, it became translucent, phasing straight through Symas's defending sword. The Ethereal Blade sliced through him, damaging something deeper than flesh.


## A Desperate Gambit

Captain Alexstrom broke away from the combat, his hand disappearing beneath his coat. A grinding sound rose from within the ship.

Three small squares on the deck split open, replaced by black metal platforms. Each bore two sheath-like structures that stood upright toward the storm-dark sky. The grinding continued from somewhere deeper in the vessel, accompanied by an alarm that the wind and rain nearly swallowed.

Alexstrom grinned. "You think the Maelstrom is behind on advancements? That we didn't learn from the war with the Garleans?" He straightened, blood running from his wounds but triumph in his voice. "You pirates are about to be the test subjects for some of our newest deterrent methods."

Two mechanical sentinels stomped up from the lower decks, making their way toward the helm.

Rove pushed himself up from the deck, bruised and bleeding from his earlier fall. He'd had enough. Consequences be damned. He raised the potato-gun-grenade and fired it at the Captain. "DO NOT DO THAT AGAIN!"

Orlando licked his lips as Symas's strikes connected. His laughter grew louder, more manic, as he slashed back into the First Mate. "Yes! Yes! Yes! Try harder! Let me see your futility!"

Something in Vesper's chest twisted at the sight of the sentinels. Not fear. Rage—so pure and incandescent it burned away every other thought. She took a step toward the approaching machine, heedless of Alexstrom now, heedless of strategy or self-preservation. "You think—" Her voice cracked. "You think you can use those things against me? AGAINST US?"

She flailed her swords against it, strikes landing almost ineffectively as memory consumed her. "They strapped us down and let their fucking machines tear us apart! And you think—"

Z'iro's blades flashed. The sentinel that had charged toward him fell in pieces, clearing his path to the Captain.

A gentle ringing became an echoed bell. Silence stilled the air around Orlando for a split second as the Dream Hare's power swept beneath and within him, invigorating. V'ika drew a card glowing blue and threw it at Orlando. Water bubbles drifted from it before the card returned to his hand.

Ugan glanced from the magitek platforms to the sentinel to the Captain. A quick shrug, and he threw his weight into a spear-thrust. The point scratched along Alexstrom's arm, not deep, but it bled.

Fiora's eyes snapped to Ayre. Aether flowed toward her in a thin stream, knitting the worst of the damage. The moment the spell was released, Fiora shoved herself out of the wheelchair. Her legs nearly buckled. She hobbled backward, away from the machines, one hand braced against the rail, the other reaching for the next handhold. The wheelchair sat abandoned behind her.

Ayre let out a growl of pain and wrath. Her axe rose to block the first strike, but others cut across her chest and arms. Blood poured from the wounds, but she wasn't giving up. She parried upward, then launched herself at the Captain and smashed her forehead into his with a brutal crack.

Alexstrom staggered, then turned and shouted down the deck to the marines still fighting—their numbers looking thin now.

"Turn the front cannon and fire!"

One marine gave him a confused look.

"DO IT NOW!"

The large cannon swung around. It opened fire across the entire length of the ship.

The same barrel that had bounced across the Vengeful Phoenix's deck earlier had somehow made it over to the Gilded Rose. It bounced high, narrowly missing Orlando again—and clobbered Skyas directly in his smart mouth.

Fiora ducked under the wheelchair she'd abandoned moments before, huddling as bullets ricocheted off metal and wood around her.

Rove fished through his bag and produced a metal can of Yak Milk. He threw it at the Captain.

Steven launched off Z'iro's shoulder, wings beating furiously. The seagull pecked at Captain Alexstrom's eyes, shrieking loud enough to cut through the cannon fire.

V'ika poured aether into his globe, then used gravity to slam the Captain down onto the deck. A heartbeat later, he yanked him back upright—ensuring everyone mid-swing wouldn't miss their target.

Ugan jolted at the cannon's roar, his free hand instinctively grabbing his horn. When the grapeshot stopped and dust settled, he looked around the peppered deck, then down at his untouched body. "Gaha! Missed me, scurvy bilge rats!" He reared back. "HEAVE... HO!" His spear flew toward the Captain.

Ayre spun her axe in a wide arc, deflecting the last of the grapeshot. She used the momentum to launch herself into the air, then brought the burning blade crashing down onto Alexstrom. Maniacal laughter tore from her throat—a woman unchained. "COME ON! WHAT COWARD NEEDS A GUN TO KILL A BEAST?! YOU CAN'T KILL ME YOURSELF?!" She pressed the attack, relentless. "WHAT WOULD YOUR ADMIRAL THINK ABOUT YOUR COWARDICE?!"


## Shattered Pieces

The rage burning through Vesper was different now. Not the playful violence from before. Not the artistic brutality she'd been savoring. This was personal.

This was every Garlean researcher who'd stood behind reinforced glass while machines tortured her. Every officer who'd signed off on the experiments. Every decorated fuck in a fancy uniform who'd thought people were acceptable test subjects.

"All you Grand Company bastards are the same!" She screamed it over the storm, closing the distance in three long strides. She fell on the sentinel like a woman possessed, katanas rising and falling in a frenzy.

Xyla pulled back on her bow. Two arrows materialized. She aimed for a breath, then loosed—both streaking toward the Captain. One buried itself in his shoulder. The other punched between his ribs. "Fucker," she muttered.

Fiora's vision tunneled. She didn't think. Light erupted from Dawnbringer, cascading outward in waves that washed over the crew. The shield settled over them. She felt each barrier lock into place, tethered to her will. She knelt on the deck, one hand white-knuckled around the staff, focused solely on maintaining what she'd wrought.

Skyas turned his attention to the Captain and plunged his blade into Alexstrom's shoulder. "By the way, just pointing out that we killed the guy that looked like me first." He twisted the blade. "I feel just a little offended."

Orlando smiled at Symas. He cut him down at the knees. The First Mate collapsed as blood spurted across the deck. Orlando's cackle rose over the storm as multiple tears opened around him, rips in reality that gaped like hungry mouths. "Mastema," he crooned, his voice like a babbling brook, "shall we make something interesting out of this corpse?"

Arms reached through the Tears. They grabbed Symas as he struggled, clawing at the deck, trying to drag himself away. Tearing. Peeling. Breaking. Snapping. Fragments of bone and patches of flesh scattered across the deck, arranged into shapes that resembled roses. Finally, the skull of a raven wearing a White Mage's cowl emerged through a tear and tore out Symas's still-beating heart from the largest flower.

Orlando surveyed the small field of roses he'd made. The grapeshot had destroyed most of them, scattering the remains to the wind. He bowed to the Avatar behind him, then chuckled as the mania faded from his eyes. "Today is a good day indeed." He looked at the Captain and raised his scythe.

Alexstrom tried to lift his saber one last time. A pathetic, trembling attempt at defense. But Vesper didn't see a Maelstrom Captain anymore. The uniform shifted in her vision—became white and black, became Garlean Imperial colors. The face became every researcher. Every officer. Every decorated monster who'd stood and watched while the machines worked.

"This is for every single one of us you bastards broke." Her voice cracked with years of buried agony. Her right katana came up in a vicious rising slash that caught him under the jaw. The steel carved through his throat, up through his mouth, splitting tongue and palate. The blade erupted through the roof of his skull in a spray of teeth and bone.

He staggered backward, drowning in his own blood.

She reversed her grip on her left blade and drove it through his chest. The katana punched through his sternum with a crack of splintering bone, through his heart, and burst out his back in a spray of crimson. She held him there. Impaled. Dying. Their faces inches apart as lightning split the sky behind them.

Rain mixed with blood. For a moment she was back in that laboratory—but this time she held the power. She decided who lived and died. "You don't get to use machines on people," Vesper whispered, watching the light fade from his eyes. "Not ever again." She ripped both blades free in opposite directions. One tore up and out through his shattered jaw. The other yanked sideways through his chest cavity, opening him completely.

The body hit the deck in pieces.

Vesper stood over it, chest heaving, drenched in gore. In the back of their shared skull, Anya was there too. Not fighting anymore. Not screaming. Just watching. Wondering how she was going to explain this later.


## The Aftermath

Z'iro looked at the fallen bodies of the Captain and First Mate. He shook his head, then moved to the wheel of the Gilded Rose and surveyed the deck below. The remaining marines lay dead among the pirates' own losses. He inspected the carnage for a long moment. Then he laughed. He raised his right sword high. "The ship's ours!" A thundering cheer rose from the crew on the lower deck.

Z'iro left the helm and moved toward the hold, preparing to descend. He opened the door, then paused. His smile faded instantly. He closed it most of the way, holding it only slightly ajar—just enough that he alone could see what lay beyond. "Fuck." His voice carried across the deck. "We are in so much trouble. Xyla, Skyas, get down here. Now."

Fiora pushed herself off the deck and staggered toward Z'iro. Her eyes slipped closed mid-step. She stumbled before she reached him, stopping to kneel and catch her breath.

Skyas raised an eyebrow. "Something you need me to see?"

V'ika stowed his globe on his back and moved to listen, tail twitching. He glanced at Fiora, then quickly crossed to her side.

Ugan walked to where his spear jutted from the deck and yanked it free, careful not to slip on the viscera. Once he'd stowed the weapon, he eyed the Captain's corpse. "That Maelstrom dog has a nice pair of boots, he does." He nudged the body with his foot. "Just have to wash out the gore."

Orlando followed behind Skyas but paused when he saw Fiora struggling. He offered her a hand. "Steady there, friend. Let us help you." Fiora allowed herself to be supported by V'ika and Orlando, going limp between them. Her eyes flickered as she fought to stay conscious.

V'ika looked around, then fixed his gaze on the abandoned wheelchair across the deck. "Someone bring the wheelchair over. Now." Orlando crossed the deck, grabbed the wheelchair, and brought it to Fiora.

The Dream Hare's staff dissipated. Light clicking of heels followed as they descended to join the rest of the crew.

Rove moved to collect his can of yak milk and the boots he'd thrown, stuffing them back into his bag. He started toward the ship, questioning how life as a merchant had turned into this.

Xyla snapped her gaze to Z'iro. "What the fuck is it now?" She grabbed a rope and slid down, landing silently. She pushed past the crew to stand beside Z'iro, glaring at him as she waited.

Ayre wiped the gore and blood from her face. A long exhale. The rage settled back into its cage, lid firmly in place before it could bubble over again. She followed the others down, a huff in her breath.

Xyla leaned close to Z'iro and whispered something only he could hear.

Z'iro looked at her. Then at Skyas. Then back at the gathered group. He smirked and swung the door open. The tip they'd received promised a five million gil haul. What lay beyond was not five million. A mountain of golden coins spilled forward as the door banged against the wall. The hold was packed front to back, nearly to the ceiling. Closer to ten million gil, easily.

"We need to start loading now," Z'iro said. "I mean right now. And when we're done, we're sinking this ship to the bottom of the sea." He turned to face the crew. "No one goes back to Limsa for the next month. Everyone lays low once we've given out shares. Do not spend it all too quickly and draw attention."

A massive payout. Not the biggest haul ever found, but huge. The crew would have their pockets filled for months to come. Z'iro looked at the mountain of gil and laughed.

Skyas grinned at the gold. "I know exactly how I'll be using this."

Z'iro turned to Xyla. "Start finding a way to launder this out once we've stuffed the manor full. The Maelstrom is going to be furious. We need to make this vanish."

Xyla's gaze moved from Z'iro to the haul. A wicked grin spread across her face. "Skyas, get them to work. Everyone gets moving." Her expression hardened. "And if they take any before it's divvied, they're going down with this ship." She clapped her hands, the sound sharp over the fading storm. "Let's get moving. You heard him. Now."
