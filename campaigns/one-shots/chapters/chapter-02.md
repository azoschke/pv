Jijiroon's trading post sat on a wooden platform built out over the shore, sheltered against a moss-covered outcrop draped with leather hides. Crates and clay pots clustered along the planks. Behind them, the lake stretched wide and flat, its surface pale in the morning light. Across the water, partway up the rise, the rooftops of Camp Bronze Lake caught the early sun.

The reinforcements came up the path from the northeast, having put in at Costa Del Sol the night prior and followed the Agelyss river inland through Wineport before crossing on foot.

They have something living kept below the ship, guarded by a Reaver crew. Those same tonic crates turned up at Memeroon's, and what he told us confirms this goes deeper. We just don't know how deep."

Across the way, Fiora set a sack of gil down where Jijiroon could see it. "What do you know of this lalafell merchant we've heard so much of?"

The Qiqirn's paws hovered over the sack, then snatched it. "Jijiroon trade no trades with little man, no, no. Little man, not Jijiroon's customer, no shinies traded, no goods exchanged. But Jijiroon see him! Jijiroon see him plenty about the hot water camp, walking with his many-haired men, very many men with very much hair." He shook his head. "Strange men, strange men."

He leaned closer over the counter, voice quivering. "Men do work in lake. At night, when good Jijiroon should be sleeping, Jijiroon look out and Jijiroon see lights. Lights in the water, deep, deep. Men in boats, men with tools, men building, building, building. What they build, Jijiroon no know." His ears flattened. "Lights move all night. Lights gone by morning. Like Jijiroon dream them, but Jijiroon no dream. Jijiroon see."

He pulled the sack toward his chest and patted it. "Jijiroon say no more. Jijiroon want long life, many shinies, no trouble with men. Travelers go now, yes? Yes."

Behind her dark lenses, Sirena's gaze flicked toward Anya. "Es nothin," she said, low and quiet. She drifted to the rear of the group and settled there.


## The Path Forward

Rose walked with her hands close to her daggers, ready to draw. Her eyes scanned the path as she went.

Swatting at insects, Ugan kept his eyes moving from one side of the road to the other. He spoke at V'ika's shoulder. "Bold when I need to be, even when assuming. But out here, far from the sea, who knows what manner of beast... man or otherwise... lurks in this muck."

Through the rainfall, Naf's eye caught on movement at Camp Bronze Lake. Even through the downpour, the activity carried clearly enough. Men unloading crates and hauling them up the hill. He turned and relayed what he had seen.

"Nothing I haven't seen before," V'ika said when he finished. He looked at the others. "Has anyone seen anything yet? Or shall we start sending a smaller group?"

Siegrid's eyes had drifted toward a distant set of ruins. Her thoughts had drifted with them.

Falling back to settle her gauntlet, Tsolom worked a strap as she walked. Her attention kept catching on the lake. She halted as she came up alongside Sirena.

Sirena had stayed at the rear. Her eyes did not watch the terrain or the structures ahead. From behind the dark lenses and the high collar that hid the lower half of her face, her gaze stayed on the group itself.

Tsolom's brow furrowed. She turned her head as if listening, then her attention came back to the group.

Primrose lifted a hand. "I will volunteer. I have two lucky rabbit's feet."

Snickering at the back-and-forth between his master and mistress Fiora, Naf turned to take stock of his quiver and the rest of his supplies. "So are we handling this stealthily, or should we just walk up and start asking questions?" His grin gave the joke away.

Tsolom looked at the rain. "Sad. I can ask questions so nicely with my punch."

Naf chuckled at Tsolom's comment.

Primrose leaned toward Evelyn. "Am I supposed to be sneaking through too?" she whispered.

Watching V'ika slip behind a tree, Ugan gauged his surroundings, then leapt for a rock and crouched behind it.

Naf walked over to a flat stone and sat down to wait. He pulled out the book he had brought and began to read.

Like my bones might end up sunken in this swampy grime for all eternity, should I fall. An ill fate indeed. Now let's find some chatty locals what know what's going on."

Not quite as fun as a naval battle, but land offers a nice variety of flavors for trouble to get into." He turned to Tsolom. "Care to tag along, Tsolom? ## Scouting Expedition

The boat sat in the distance by the ruins, a small craft drawn close to shore. Men in red shirts moved along its deck, hauling crates one and two at a time. The closer the scouting party got, the louder the humming in the air became, a low constant note.

Crouching at the shallows, Primrose reached for a small salamander wallowing in the silt. "Slimy," she announced, with the satisfaction of a name well chosen. She got two strokes of its head before it twisted around and clamped down on her hand. By the time she yelped, it had wriggled free and bolted off into deeper water, vanishing in a swirl of mud.

Siegrid let the quieter of them lead. Her eyes stayed on the ship; her ears went elsewhere. "Wait. They have it?" She glanced sidelong at the others. "Just so we're clear, everyone hears the loud humming, right?"

"Yes, I can hear it too," Evelyn whispered. She looked between Siegrid and Primrose. "Do you want me to go first and check things out, or are we approaching those men directly?"

"Hmm." Siegrid weighed it. "The question is, is it tech or some odd monster or something. But as for your question, Evelyn, I could always approach them in my jolly manner, and you hang back and act as needed."

Another salamander came sniffing up to where they stood. Its mouth opened, slowly.

Primrose tilted her head. "I've got a song in my head. Surely I haven't been humming it..."

Evelyn blinked. "Hmm. Okay, let's get going."

"I'm not an expert in anything," Primrose added, "except perhaps charming people with my charisma."

The salamander snapped at the air as they shifted forward.

Clapping one hand into her fist, Siegrid grinned. "Right then, I'll go say hello. And Tenebes, you keep quiet, eh? Don't want to scare the man yet."

The sword at her hip huffed.

Ahead, a large Roegadyn stood near the gangplank, watching the work with a steady eye. A stream of men in pairs and threes lugged crates onto the bank, each one bearing the same smirking lalafell stamped on its side. The men hauled their cargo toward the camp, up the stairs, and away to the left.

Evelyn caught Siegrid's elbow. "Oh, wait, Siegrid. There's a lot of them, and the small one. Maybe we should contact the others about it first." Her hand lifted to her ear, activating the linkpearl. "Hey, guys, there are a lot of men on the boat moving crates, and a Lalafell seems to be supervising all of it."

Shaking her head, Siegrid set off in a happy trot and threw a wave back, as if to say wait here. "Nah, we got this. And if it goes south, we just nab the little guy as a hostage."

Evelyn brought a hand to her face and let out a breath. She glanced at Primrose. "Be careful." She drifted forward at a slower pace, working herself into the local traffic.

The men were too busy with their work to take much note of her, passing her off as another idle soul wandered out from the springs.

Closing on the Roegadyn at the boat, Siegrid raised her voice in cheerful greeting. "Hey, chaps, what're you all doing out here when the party sounds like it's over there? Don't seem fair they got you all on guard duty or something."

The Roegadyn turned toward her. He ran a hand through a set of thick, luscious locks atop his head. "We've got work to do. Get'cher self on back to the Springs and don' go bothering my men. Ye' tourists don' mind yer own business."

Evelyn slipped along the edge of the work, eyes catching at the side of a crate without seeming to look.

Siegrid kept her grin in place. "Come on now, mate, no need to be like that. If you're all that strapped with work, maybe you could use an extra set of hands. The rest of you all can go join the party yonder."

One of the men yelled across to the Roegadyn. "Puny, what do you want wit' dis 'ere crate? Got the rations for that 'dere thing."

Puny's attention pulled away. He answered Siegrid only as he turned to go. "If yer lookin' for work, go 'n see the boss at the Springs."

Three against this many was not the math Siegrid wanted, and pushing further would only earn a fight. She kept the friendly grin up. "Might just do that, then, sir. Can you tell me what he looks like and his name, as well as your name, so he knows you sent me?"

One of the workers shouldered a crate and pointed at the lalafell stamp on its side. "Can't miss 'em. Ask for Chachajoni Lalajoni. De' boss 'ere is Puny Whimper. Giv' em that name an' e' might have a question or two for ye."

While they spoke, Evelyn drifted closer. The hum was not coming from the crates. It carried up from somewhere deeper in the water, beneath the boat. The crates themselves gave off a different note, a sickly sweetness that turned in the back of her throat.

Siegrid waved at the worker. "Hey, thanks, mate. If I get the job, I owe you a pint." She turned and walked off, not hurrying. Once the boat was a safe distance behind, she motioned for Evelyn and Primrose to follow.

Evelyn's hand twitched toward the lid of a passing crate, but she did not let it land. She turned and fell in with Siegrid.

A few steps along, Siegrid lifted her hand to her ear. "Hmmm, hello, this thing is on, right? Right. All — got some info that might help. The lalafell mentioned earlier, we got a name. Chachajoni Lalajoni. He's the boss, seems to be over where you are. Just ask around for him. Boss here at the boat is Puny Whimper. Heading back over to join up."

She turned to Evelyn with a smile, fangs showing. "See what did I tell you. Everything went fine."

"Right," Evelyn said with her own small smile. "I forgot that sweet-talking is a terrifying weapon."

Reaching into her pocket, Siegrid drew out a bottle of Ut'ohmu Chili Sauce. She took a swig, capped it, and tucked it back. "Few things a upbeat attitude can't work its magic on."

## Questioning in Camp Bronze Lake


### The Armorer: Ugan and Anya

Approaching the armorer's stall, Ugan put on a sailor's cheer. "Ahoy, mate. We've a couple come in from out Limsa way, come to enjoy the baths and lake and all. Seems a bit more... tense than I expected."

The armorer tossed his rag aside. "Limsa, eh? Should've stayed there. Springs are getting quite busy these last couple of weeks. Hard to relax. If you're just here for the water, soak and leave. If you're buying, tell me what you're needin'."

Crossing his arms, Ugan held the man's gaze. "Oi, I thought this were the prime vacation spot of all the isles... yet you're acting like you've got a rotten fish smacked across your jaw. Too many people, is it? Too much business for you? What are you, daft?"

Anya reached out and laid a hand on Ugan's arm. "Relax. I'm sure this man is just worked up. No need to cause a scene, darling." Her attention shifted to the armorer. "If there's tension here, if people are getting hurt or there's danger we should know about..." She let the implication hang. "We're not looking for trouble. We're just trying to understand what's happening so we can stay safe." Her fingers traced the edge of her star globe.

At her touch, Ugan lowered his arms, but his teeth stayed clenched and his stare did not move from the armorer's face.

A sharp gesture sent them off. "If you want sunshine and smiles, go find a beach in Costa del Sol. Unless you're looking to buy a new chestpiece or drop some gil, I've got no patience for tourists with sharp tongues."

Anya reached into her coin purse and drew out a stack of gil. She set it on the counter between them, fingers resting lightly atop the coins. "We're not tourists," she said, the softness gone from her voice. "And we're not here to cause you trouble. But clearly something's made this place tense enough that even talking about it isn't worth your time." She nudged the stack forward. "So let's make it worth your time." Her eyes held his.

Squinting, Ugan unclenched his fist and gave a small nod toward Anya. "Listen to the lady, mate. You'll get richer for it."

For a long moment, the armorer stared at the gil. His calloused hand slid forward, covered the stack, and swept it off the counter into his apron in one motion. Leaning over a dented pauldron, he made a show of inspecting a scratch. "Look, if the air feels heavy, it's 'cause of the extra weight being hauled. North. We don't like the tension. It's bad for business and worse for sleep. But nobody here is keen on being the one to ask why. Ain't got nothin' more to say."

A small nod from Anya. "Of course. You've been most generous with your time." A short pause. "We'll leave you to your work." She turned and started toward the others, hands folded.

### The Private: Skyas and Tsolom

"Very well, I'll have you as back-up." Skyas smirked at Tsolom and turned toward an off-duty Storm Private. "The storm certainly has less bite here than it does out at sea, eh? And yet somehow I miss the chaos."

Easy and innocent, Tsolom kept her stance near Skyas, ready to play the part. The crackle of Rose's voice over the linkshell pulled at her ear. She glanced at Skyas to gauge whether he had caught it, then pressed on. "I thought people would be happy to be at calm here. Hot springs are so relaxing."

A short, dry laugh from the private. "Oh, the water is fine for the first hour. But after a week of watching people soak while you're pulling double shifts, the novelty wears thin. Too much movement for a 'relaxing' spot."

"I hear that, brother." A sly nod from Skyas confirmed for Tsolom that he had heard Evelyn's report. "Fair, but hardly relaxing when you're on the clock, y'know? Nothing truly puts the mind at ease quite like the open sea, even when working. But, that's just a perk of the job sometimes. Gotta go where the coin's at." His eye drifted back to the private, watching for the reaction.

Hooking her arm through Skyas's, Tsolom let out a sigh. "With all the talk we still have not gone to the bath or do anything." She tugged him along.


### The Merchant: Vika and Naf

V'ika gripped Naf's jacket. There was little strength behind it. His head did not turn as he spoke, and his voice came barely above a breath. "Thank you... If there's... someone else you may know... that would be great to hear."

Turning to V'ika with a crumpling face, Naf said, "Master, I'm so sorry it looks like they've nothing to help us." Even if it isn't health related, we are willing to try anything." He swiped at his eyes, where no tears were. "Aye, or even someone you could point us to?"

A glance from Naf to V'ika asked the next move.

"Any small tonic is useful," V'ika said. "We'll be moving on and stop bothering you." He patted Naf's arm.

Letting Naf step off first, V'ika followed close behind. As they moved, his voice stayed low. "If we have to, we can try again later, but for now we keep moving and try other merchants." His hand found his bag, and he tucked the tonic inside.


### The Reaver: Sirena and Cassian

Silent again, Sirena let her eyes move over the lake, the tables, the people. "I dohn like et," she whispered. She stepped further in, ears lowered against the brim of her hat. A glance over her right shoulder, a small nod. "You dohn like et either," she whispered.

A low growl from Sirena. She took a step back. "Buh..." Her gaze went to Cassian, then to the Reaver, then back to Cassian. "Aye..." She turned on her right heel as if to step off, then stopped. Her mouth opened, then closed. She fell in behind Cassian and made her way back down the stairs to the rest of the group.

## Reconvening

Lifting his hand to the linkshell, Cassian answered Siegrid's report. "Understood. We've seen enough here to know there's something going on. Don't linger. Gather the others and pull back to the entrance of the camp. We'll meet you there to piece this together."

When the scouting party had rejoined the rest, V'ika spoke without turning his head. "Seems you three have returned. I was busy being blind. Didn't go as well as I hoped, but there was an odd behavior with the merchant."

Padding up directly behind him, Fiora spoke in his ear. "Odd behavior?"

V'ika tilted his head, though not quite toward her. "Quickly avoided a question about if there was anyone else we could ask, and stated he was just a simple salt merchant or something like that. His body stiffened as well when we asked."

Resting a hand against her chin, Evelyn turned the report over. "There was a strange humming down there... coming from underwater. That's odd."

A grunt from Fiora. "We noticed the same thing out at the Isle of Umbra before we saw the creature on the ship. Was too busy trying to rescue a sailor to look too closely."

V'ika nodded toward Fiora and turned as Cassian spoke.

"We found something." Cassian's gaze went to Sirena, opening the floor.

Caught off guard, Sirena blinked. Her right arm curled at the elbow, her thumb cocked back over her shoulder toward the stairs. "Et stinks up there. Buh noh like a normal stink. Et smells like work, an lots oh et. An theres a guard, tha aint normal. This es a public place."

Tsolom came up with Skyas and let go of his arm, listening, working it through.

"Didn't learn too much of value on our end," Skyas said, "only that the guards here are babysitting, essentially. Seems they haven't had much trouble on their end."

"Or that they are looking away," Tsolom added.

A small grin from Skyas. "Likely both, if we're being honest."

Looking around the gathered group, Anya laid out what she had. "The merchant we spoke to gave us a bit to work with. There is movement north, significant enough that he called it extra weight being hauled, which from an armorer means equipment, not people. He is unhappy about it, but not involved in it. And he is afraid to ask questions, which means whoever is moving north is the kind of party that discourages curiosity. Whatever is moving north is moving through a specific route, and the people along that route have made a collective decision not to notice it."

Evelyn looked between them. "So, do we assume we've reached the point where we need to dig deeper into this? It's all suspicious, but I guess we need proof."

Ugan leaned in toward Anya and dropped his voice to a whisper in her ear.

Naf shook his head. "By the time we had heard your message, we had already left the merchant we were talking to." He sighed and turned to V'ika with a nod, then back to Siegrid. "He did seem a mite weird." He chewed on it for a moment. "I'm half tempted to just shout Chachajoni Lalajoni as loud as I can and see what happens." A chuckle followed.

Letting out a sigh, Siegrid had been hoping for more given what the dockworker had said. "Hmmm, maybe someone had back then. See if the names Puny Whimper or Chachajoni Lalajoni mean anything to him. I'll go with whoever does, since the ones at the ship think I'm looking for work. And maybe the others could see if the Yellowjackets nearby can be of use for once and ask them if the names ring any bells."

A nod from Naf. "Aye, I hope so. If not, then I guess we'll have to split up again and start asking around." He paused. "Though that might make us look even more suspicious."

"Want to show me to the merchant, then?" Siegrid asked. "I'll ask him, say Puny Whimper sent me to find a bloke named Chachajoni Lalajoni. You by chance heard of him? Said he might have work for me. Sound good to you, Naf?"

"Sure, I can do that," Naf said. "Follow me." He walked toward the stall, keeping his distance, and gave a small nod toward the merchant. "That's them." Then, lower, to Siegrid, "Be careful. I'll be over here just in case."


### Return to the Merchant: Naf and Siegrid

Sauntering up to the merchant, Siegrid kept her smile easy and friendly. "Evening there, sir. Might you maybe help me. I'm trying to track someone down. Bloke named Chachajoni Lalajoni. Was told he might have work for me. Word of a man goes by the name Puny Whimper sent me. Might have a few coin for you if you can point me in the right direction?"

At the name, the merchant's eyes went glassy. "I can't run a business with tha' smuck takin' et all." He grabbed at the thinning hair at his temples. "Nae, I can't stand for it. No, I tell ye', I won't stand for it. Take yerself elsewhere."

Siegrid softened her voice. "Now hold on, sir. I think it might help you to help me. I'm trying to track the man down to do something about him. Join up, get some proof on him in the act. Maybe gotta play the role, though, you know. So you sure you can't point him out, knowing that now?"

The merchant looked up at her through tearful lashes. "Nae, miss. Cannae trust anyone. Yer just another one o' them. Begone. Leave me to my sorrows."

Siegrid shrugged and turned away, shaking her head. "Fair, sir. I'll get the job done without you."


## Hair-Raising Work

"We're still missing a couple of members," V'ika said. "Let's make a plan once they return."

Cassian glanced back toward the guards, then to the group. "I'm tired of the sneaking. If these Reavers want to play soldier in a spa, I say let's give them a reason to break a sweat. We want to know what's in the crates? Let's crack a few bones and see what spills."

Sirena rolled her right shoulder, her left hand coming across to tighten the plate again. The bones crunched as the joint moved. "Yah knoh..." She thought for a beat, turned on her heel, and looked up the stairs once more. A sigh. "Ne'ermind." Her eyes came back to Cassian. "Tah be clear? I cahn hurt'em nohw?"

"Yes! Finally!" Tsolom clapped her fist into her palm.

V'ika exhaled. "Hm... fine. We done enough to figure we should just attack by this point."

A wide smile from Evelyn as her hands settled over her daggers. "At your command."

Reaching back for the haft of his trident, Ugan gave Anya a nod. "Aye, aye, let's run through some of these codfish and fry them up for supper. Not literally, of course... that would be cannibalism. I don't partake in cannibalism. Well... not unless under dire circumstances. Which this isn't."

A grin spread across Naf's face as he readied his bow and quiver. "At your command, boss." He nodded to Cassian as he saw others around him doing the same.

Siegrid rejoined the group and tossed her own findings into the pool. "Right, so that merchant Naf pointed me to, he knows what's going on here and where we can find Chachajoni Lalajoni. But he's not talking. Where he is, or who he is. Man's too scared and upset about what is happening."

Fiora drew a breath. "Where his goods are, he'll be. Did anyone see anything, any movement?" Her foot tapped against the stone as she looked the lot of them over.

"There es a strong stench oh sweat up tha stairs," Sirena said, "an'ah guard tha won'leh yeh go nearer tah the build'in. Does tha count?"

"A good enough start as any," Fiora replied. "Does anyone have any solid leads on the merchandise?"

"Aye, something about it being moved north," Ugan said.

Fiora's gaze pinned him. "North from where? I haven't seen hide nor hair of any crates, not since we left the advertisements in the cove."

He glanced at Anya and shrugged.

"That I am afraid he did not divulge," Anya said. "Only that it appears to be significant enough to cause the merchants here distress and loss of sleep."

Cracking her neck, Siegrid weighed the options. "Sounds like the best way forward is forcing our way up them stairs then. Unless we're going to make that merchant Naf showed me talk."

Fiora turned on her heel and headed in the direction Sirena had come from. Sirena fell in behind her, the grin behind the high collar of her coat hidden from view, eyes searching for the Reaver who had stopped her before.

As the group reached the upper landing, one of the guards stepped out to meet them. "Boss said no visitors, begon' wit' ye'. Unless ye' be pickin' up an order, be off."

"I suppose we want answer and not just a pile of body," Tsolom said.

Skyas rested his hand on the hilt of his blade and tilted his head one way and the other to crack his neck. The standing around had been wearing on him.

The guard bristled. "I don' know who ye' think ye' are. But ye' have some nerve threatenin' this place o' business." His hand settled on his axe. "I assume yer not 'ere to pick up yer goods."

Siegrid stepped up with her grin still on. "Hold now, big boy. Your man Puny Whimper said you might have work, and told us to check with the boss."

The guard's hand faltered. "Puny sent ye', did 'e?" His eye traveled over their attire — none of them dressed for bathing. "An' wat qualifi... qualifici..." He spat. "Wat makes ye' suited fer it?"

While he spoke, Sirena drew off the dark glasses V'ika had made for her, folded the arms, and tucked them into her coat. She stepped up beside Fiora. The gold that had filled her eyes was gone; in its place sat a soft void blue. She turned her face toward the baths. "I ahm gettin hungr'eh."

"Should we start stripping or...?" Naf whispered.

Tsolom stretched her face into the most innocent smile she had in her, as though she could undo the threat she had made just by smiling brightly enough.

Siegrid nodded along. "That he did. Said go check with Chachajoni Lalajoni. Figure your man Puny must have seen we're fit for the job. Think you'll find we're quite versatile, be it combat or hard labor. Isn't that right, all?"

Sirena's right arm rose, the shoulder crunching as it lifted, then curled at the elbow as her hand waved. "Aye. Labor, fight'in, whae'er yah say."

V'ika kept his blindfold in place. "I can work as long I am guided. I am much stronger than one thinks."

Patting his bicep, Ugan said, "Aye, we can work alright. Any job you please... no questions asked. Just a crew of workers what need jobs, that be us."

The guard's hand slid off his axe. "Yer seem alright fer a bunch o' 'lubbers. Boss is inside. Get yer orders from 'em personally." His eyes went over the tops of their heads. "He'll make sure te' set ye' up wit some tonic. Yer lookin' awfully thin on top."

Giving the man a friendly wave, Siegrid moved past with a wink. "You're alright too, big boy. Maybe we talk later, hmm."

Ugan's teeth ground audibly. "'Lubber?! Me, a lubber?! That flat-bellied seaworm flopping about in the muck sucking down sand... me, a 'lubber..."

A small smile tugged at Anya's mouth. "Not just a lubber, Ugan. A _land_ lubber. I would not suffer such an insult if I were you. Then again, I prefer the more refined things, so I will content myself with the land."

Skyas placed a hand on the back of Ugan's shoulder. "At ease, friend." His voice dropped to a whisper. "He'll eat his words in time."

V'ika moved closer to Naf, using him as his guide.

"Let's go in before someone say something stupid," Tsolom said with a chuckle.

Ugan's brow furled as he glanced from Anya to Skyas. "Oh, I'll be suffering nothing... just you wait... he'll know the fury of the crimson tide, he will. Just you wait."

## The Mastermind

The room was half-filled with crates stacked nearly to the ceiling. At its center, a ruddy lalafell sat at a table, vials of a sweet-smelling green liquid arranged before him and an entourage at his back. One of them said something at his shoulder, and he laughed, loud and unhurried. Some present recognized him from Aleport. Others recognized the smirk from the side of every crate in the room.

Fiora's voice came at a mutter. "It's a small wonder the proprietor allows this. What it must do to his business. What sort of cut is he getting?"

Outside, Naf had counted the men with thick, luscious hair — thirty, perhaps forty around the camp. Inside, a handful more. The same sweet odor that hung on the men outdoors clung to the men in here too, thick enough that V'ika tugged his blindfold up to cover his nose without revealing his eyes.

Cassian stood back, axe within reach, and let Siegrid talk.

"It's amazing, isn't it, what people will do for enough coin," Siegrid said, ambling toward the table. "Speaking on coin, we hear you might have work for us. Assuming you're Chachajoni Lalajoni. Your man Puny Whimper sent us over. Thought we might be the help you all could use."

Tsolom held her tongue this time, ready at the shoulder.

Ugan's hand drifted back to the haft of his trident.

Chachajoni's brow furrowed, then smoothed at their recognition of his name. He swept his hands wide. "Ah, how my illustrious name spreads about the ignorant masses." He kicked his stubby legs and dropped from the chair, landing in a small plume of dust. "There is always work for idle hands. Tell me, have you perused my wares?"

"Aye, we've seen your wares' handiwork on the way in," Naf chimed in. "My, my. I almost swooned at the sight of so many with such thick, luscious hair." A hand to his forehead, a small mock sigh.

"Yes, that we have," Siegrid added with a nod. "Fuller heads of hair we have never seen. Can only imagine what it might do for fur."

Tsolom looked down at a strand of her own hair. Her brow drew in. She blinked at it.

V'ika's tail moved in close behind Naf.

"Oh ho ho." Chachajoni's grin spread, quirking higher on one side until it matched the smirk on the crates exactly. "Now, you must give it a try." He bustled to the table, gathered the vials, and began passing them to those present.

Cassian shook his head and showed his palm to refuse. He did not stop the others.

Ugan ran a hand through his own hair beneath his bandana and shrugged. "Can't hurt... I am getting up there in summers, and last thing I want is to lose my locks. Hand here, here." He took the vial and tipped it back in a single swallow.

The tonic took effect at once. Follicles thickened across his scalp by the second, a tingle running over the skin. As he drank deeper, something else came with it, a long, mournful call rising from beneath the lake, threading through his head. His eyes drifted toward the door. He opened his mouth to speak of it. Nothing came out.

Naf turned with his vial in hand as if to show V'ika. "Oh look, Master V'ika, we get to try it ourselves." In the same motion, his fingers worked the swap; the vial that reached V'ika was not the one Chachajoni had given him, but a merchant's tonic from earlier.

Tsolom uncorked her vial and brought it under her nose. The scent hit harder than expected — sea air, distant song, something pulling under it. The lalafell's voice from across the room suddenly felt more reasonable. Her resistance softened.

Siegrid hesitated. Cassian had said not to drink. But the moment was on her now. She lifted the vial, took a sip, and gave a small bow. "Thank you kindly, sir."

The same sensation rose along her scalp. The follicles thickened. The mournful call from the water reached for her too.

V'ika tipped back the swapped tonic without hesitation, eyes hidden, blindfold in place.

Ugan's hand opened. The empty vial clattered to the floor. He ran both hands through his hair, chuckling. The chuckle stretched, then trailed off. He turned toward the door with a glazed stare. He murmured something that did not quite become words and started walking.

Naf swallowed his own mismatched tonic, winked toward V'ika, and turned back to Chachajoni. "Ahhh, thank you kindly, sir. I wonder how long, though, until we see results?"

The lalafell's smile dipped. "Perhaps something was wrong with the composition. Some impurity. I won't stand for it. Please, try another. Drink deep." A weight had settled into his voice, low and pressing.

Ugan turned mid-step and rushed back to the table. He grabbed a second vial and tipped it down his throat without slowing.

"Where are you going, U?" Cassian's voice cut sharp. He saw Ugan's hand on the second vial and his expression fixed. He lowered his voice for the others. "Don't drink more of it."

Tsolom drew in a breath. "Don't drink..." But the lalafell's voice slid back over hers, and her eyes went to the vial in her own hand. The reasoning came easy. Why not.

Naf moved fast, hooking an arm around Ugan's. "I think we've had more than enough." A tight smile at the lalafell. "I think it's working just fine. I feel my hair growing as we speak."

Ugan twitched against Naf's grip. His head pulled toward the doorway, toward the rain falling beyond. He said something low and shapeless and tried to push back toward it.

Siegrid started to step back. The sword at her hip spoke up, sharp enough that she stilled mid-motion. _"Hold it, you dumb cat. Where do you think you're going. You feed me aether. I don't want more of that swill. Don't you dare take another sip."_

"Hey, stop that, you blasted sword," Siegrid muttered. "Alright, alright, I won't drink no more. But what if we took a walk outside, huh?"

_"Fight it out, so help me, I will devour all your aether right here."_

V'ika stayed at Naf's side, hand on his arm, the blind man act unbroken.

Tsolom brought the vial to her mouth and began to drink.

Chachajoni lifted a vial of his own and toasted them. His good humor came back full. "It would be a shame if one were to _sink_ into despair over the state of their hair. The masses will sing my praises as the markets are overtaken. You understand now why it must _forfeit_ all other competition. It will take its place on top at the _death_ of the last of the false merchants."

The emphasized words rang with something borrowed, the same long mournful note from the water carried under each one.

Cassian's voice cut through the room. "Do not let Ugan walk away. Something's wrong with him." His hand snapped out, knocking the vial from Tsolom's grip before she could finish it. He turned to Chachajoni. "You're a maniac." His axe came free of its rest as he closed the distance.


## Into the Fray

The table splintered and flipped as the axe came down. Chachajoni rolled clear of the killing arc by inches. "Kill them!" he bellowed.

Around him, a blue cast settled into his skin. Around fifteen henchmen moved as one. Steel rasped from scabbards, and the men who had been laughing a breath ago fanned out to surround the Vanguard.

Naf's bow came up. The first arrow took a guard in the throat.

Anya's fingers moved over her cards. She fed what she could into V'ika's casting, working to pull the tonic's hold off Ugan.

Sirena stepped through the door behind her. Her eyes swept the room as the chaos opened up. "All yeh had tah doh was kill'em," she said, the voice low and pleased. "An I would'ah stayed ahind and made sure the mess was cleaned up."

Her right arm rose. The warscythe came off her back, ruby eyes flaring along the haft as emerald fire raced down its length. She set it spinning around her in a wide bladed arc.

Tsolom was not done arguing about the slap. Half the vial was in her. The lalafell's voice hung at the edge of her thinking. She shook her head hard, snapped her claws open in one motion, and drove her fist into the nearest henchman's gut to clear her path.

Siegrid's blade came free with a flourish. _"Finally, a meal!"_ Tenebes called as the dice in the blade rolled. A dragon's head of green wind aether erupted from the steel — and instead of the henchman she had aimed for, the spell turned mid-flight and slammed into Anya, knocking her sideways. Anya bit down on a sound. She held her ground and kept the magic flowing into V'ika's spell.

"Tenebes, was that you messing with me right now?" Siegrid hissed.

Ugan thrashed against the rope Naf had bound him in. His hands clawed at his head. He shouted in something that wasn't language, fighting whatever still held him.

Sirena's whirlwind cleaved through the chest of one of the closer henchmen, emerald flames trailing behind the blade. Naf loosed two more arrows, finding throats. Tsolom's claws moved in a quick blur and dropped two more guards onto the wood. Cassian swung in heavy arcs at the lalafell, but Chachajoni darted under his strikes, smaller and quicker than his bulk suggested.

A blade swept low for V'ika's flank. Naf turned into it and took the cut across his ribs, then drove a kick into the henchman's chest and stepped back into his shooting stance. "Fuck, that hurt."

Tenebes rolled the dice again. A second dragon's head, this one purple lightning, erupted from Siegrid's blade — and again the strike turned mid-air. It crossed the room and struck for Sirena.

"Don't blame this on me, you wield me, you choose where the attack goes," Tenebes snapped at her.

Sirena's void-blue eyes narrowed as the strike turned her way. "Well, shite... I did'na expect tah get tha' far." A laugh went out of her. She drove the butt of the warscythe into the floorboards and held the spinning blades around her. Her left hand lifted. An orb of void aether shot back across the room toward Siegrid. "I cahn throw shite too!" Then her right shoulder gave a loud crack and she dropped to one knee, her left hand clamping over the joint with a growl through clenched teeth.

A henchman caught Ugan with a heavy swing. Blood spilled bright across his red skin. He howled, animal and surprised.

V'ika's globe pulsed. A card spun loose from its surface and orbited Ugan, scattering small showers of starlight across him. The pull of the lake's call snapped. Ugan's thrashing slowed by degrees until he stilled, blinking around the room.

"Wha..? Where's the... huh?" His hand went to his head. "My mind were addled with that foul brew..!"

Tsolom broke into a sprint. She cut between two henchmen and went straight for the lalafell, claws out, mouth open. "You little—!"

The doors to the building banged inward. Skyas came through them with his blade in hand, panting, several minor cuts along his arms. "Seems you guys could use a hand for cleaning house... I got some free time now that the yardwork is done." He gestured back over his shoulder with the smirk in place. Outside, a dozen or so half-dead reinforcements lay in a heap.

"We're being attacked, Ugan, we could really use your help right about now." Naf nocked another arrow as he reached down to cut Ugan free.

Ugan came up with the trident already in his hands. He swung it around and drove the points into the belly of the nearest foe.

Sirena pulled herself back to her feet. The warscythe came off the floor with her. Her body became a tornado of emerald fire. The blade reaped through five henchmen in a single arc. As another guard's sword came down for Anya, Sirena threw herself into the path of the strike and took the cut across her side.

V'ika's magic surged. The cleansing reached fully through Ugan and snapped the last of the tonic's grip. Naf's bow dropped another guard. Ugan's trident punched through the bellies of two more men closing on him. Tsolom finally connected with the lalafell — her fist hit his mouth with a wet crunch that sent teeth across the floor and left him screaming through a mask of blood.

Fiora's patience cracked. "I am tired of seeing your smarmy face." A spike of stone tore up through the floorboards beneath the lalafell and drove through him.

"Be rid of the henchmen! They're doing too much to us!" V'ika's globe pulsed again. A second card spun loose and orbited Siegrid, starlight dropping over her to break the tonic's hold.

Tsolom laughed at the sound the lalafell made. The earth spike caught her by surprise; she looked at it for a beat, then kicked the impaled body once, just to feel it.

Ugan dropped the trident's blades to shin-height and swept them across the legs of the men closing in.

Skyas drove into the cluster of henchmen still going for Anya. "I'll buy you some time, Anya. Try to recover a bit."

Anya pulled aether through her star globe and turned it inward. A few of the worst cuts began to close. Her gaze flicked to Skyas. A small nod.

Siegrid drew the blade back, the haze lifting fully now. She rolled onto the balls of her feet. "Think you'd take me for a ride, well, let's see how you like this." The dice in the hilt rolled and a purple dragon coiled along the steel itself. "Go, Fulmen, let's light him up!" She drove the electrified blade toward the lalafell where he hung on the spike of stone.

The remaining henchmen who still had the will to fight died quickly under the converging blades. Chachajoni slumped along the spike that held him. "My... dreams... my... empire..."

The blue aura that had wrapped him faded to nothing. The remnants drifted off his skin and slipped back into the vials still rolling on the floor. From the lake outside, the mournful cry rose up to a crescendo, hung at its peak, and then was gone. As the light went out of him, the wounds along his body began to close. He stopped moving anyway, eyes open, mouth slack.

Out the windows, more henchmen had been arriving. They reached the edge of the doorway, took in what had happened to those inside, and turned and ran.

Fiora nudged the body with her foot. "What should we do with him?"

"Tie the scallywag to driftwood and chuck him out to sea covered in chum!" Ugan kept one hand at his head as he said it. Blood stained his shirt across the side.

Fiora's mouth pulled flat. "Might get a better haul turning him in to the authorities."

"Humpf... I should have punched harder..." Tsolom looked down at the catatonic lalafell with mild disappointment.

Sirena slid the warscythe back onto her shoulder rig with her right hand, her left clamped tight over the same shoulder. She turned for the exit without speaking.

Siegrid stepped forward, fingers running along the flat of her blade. "If none are opposed, Tenebes would enjoy feasting on his aether to finish him off."

V'ika put his globe away and turned his face toward Naf. "May I ask why you choose to take hits?" His tone was curious.

Skyas slid his katana home with a long breath. "Turn him in to the Maelstrom, along with the other guards on his payroll, in my opinion. Like Fiora said, we're more likely to get a reward and clear out a few scumbags from the military while we're at it." He brushed at a stain on his shirt and frowned at it.

Tsolom looked over for Sirena and found her already moved. Her brow pulled together. She walked to the door and stopped beside Cassian, looking out.

V'ika moved to Naf and began the small work of healing through his wounds. "You are also not fit with heavy armor either."

Siegrid sheathed her blade. "Sorry, Tenebes, vote's out on him being your meal." A glance to V'ika. "And thanks, by the way, for cleansing me. I have a bad habit of trying things. Not sure this time will make me stop any more than the last, though."

Naf waved V'ika off. "Yeah, I know. But if you or Anya got taken out, then we really would've been in a pickle." He took a long swallow from his flask and let out a sigh as the magic worked into the cut along his side. "Ahh, thanks, Master, I appreciate it."

Sirena stepped out into the rain. She walked past Cassian with one hand still pressed to her shoulder. Those void eyes tilted up to the sky for a long moment. A small smile crossed her face. She kept walking. As she reached the top of the stairs, her form thinned and became mist, and then was nothing at all.

Ugan blinked hard a few times. "This little shark has given me such a splitting headache. It's as though he filled my noggin with fog, then sucked it all out at once with a straw. Whatever we do, he better suffer for it. Better suffer well."

V'ika's mouth pulled at one corner. "At least you are not dead."

Skyas headed toward the door and bumped Cassian lightly with his elbow as he passed. "Sounds like we're about wrapped up here. The decision on what to do with the little shit is y'alls. Whatever reinforcements he had on the outside are unconscious. No need to keep watch, friend. I held the line for those of you inside." He turned to Anya. "I expect to see you in the infirmary getting treated soon, Anya."

Anya pressed her hand against her side and walked from the building with as much carriage as she could hold. She gave Cassian and Tsolom a small nod, then lifted her hand. The aether folded around her into a twisted portal, and she was gone.


## Aftermath

A Maelstrom officer on duty took the man and the report. Cassian laid out the series of events, beginning with Aleport and ending in the Sanitorium. The officer made her notes, asked a few questions, and confirmed that the Maelstrom would take custody of Chachajoni and his men. On the matter of compensation, she folded her hands and said the Admiral's office would need to weigh the value of the bounty. She would send word.

Back at the Sanitorium, they set the strongbox they had pulled from Chachajoni's quarters down on the wood and worked the lid open. The lid lifted on a heap of gil. They counted it out across the floorboards and divided shares.
