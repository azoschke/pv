The morning sun cast long shadows across the beach as the Phoenix Vanguard disembarked from their ship. Salt spray misted over the dark sand where waves lapped against the shore, leaving foam that hissed and vanished. Seabirds wheeled overhead, their cries sharp against the distant crash of surf.

The woman clutched a sigil in her palm, its surface warm against her skin. The artifact tugged at her consciousness like a lodestone, drawing her gaze inexorably toward the distant heights where something ancient and powerful called out across the landscape. She felt its pull in her bones, a constant pressure that made standing still impossible.

"It's coming from up there," she murmured, pointing toward the mountain's craggy face. The sigil's warmth intensified, confirming what her instincts already knew.

The group formed up around her, weapons checked and supplies secured. Their boots crunched against the pebbled shore as they began their ascent, following game trails that wound between weathered boulders and stunted trees. The pulsing grew stronger with each step, a vibration that seemed to resonate through the very stone beneath their feet.

Hours passed before they reached the first cave mouth, a dark opening carved into the mountainside like a wound in the rock. The temperature dropped as they entered, their footsteps echoing off damp walls while something moved in the shadows beyond their torchlight.

Creatures stirred in the darkness, dark things that turned their faces toward The woman but made no move to attack. They watched, their attention fixed on the sigil she carried, as if recognizing something in its glow. The group pressed forward, weapons ready but unchallenged, until daylight beckoned from the cave's exit.

The mountain's face stretched above them as they emerged, wind whipping through their hair and clothes. The sigil's pull had grown urgent now, directing them toward another opening higher up the slope. This second cave felt different—the air itself seemed thinner, harder to breathe, as if something vital had been drained from the very atmosphere.

D'vaari pushed ahead of the group, drawn by some instinct of his own, and what they found beyond defied comprehension. The cavern opened into a vast space where reality itself had been torn apart. An enormous orange portal dominated the far wall, its surface rippling like disturbed water as energy coursed through its frame. Beyond the shimmering barrier, an alien landscape stretched endlessly beneath a crimson moon that hung like a wound in the sky.

A massive creature writhed before a portal, its tentacled form weaving patterns in the air as it channeled aether into the rift. The beast seemed oblivious to their presence, entirely focused on its own work on the otherworldly gateway.

The battle that followed shook the cavern walls. Explosions of twisted energy lit the darkness as the creature leaped and struck, its alien power warping the very air around them. But the Phoenix Vanguard held their ground, weapons flashing in the portal's orange glow until the beast finally fell, its form dissolving into wisps of corrupted aether.

In the aftermath, as they caught their breath and tended their wounds, Soot discovered something among the creature's remains—a compass-like device that pulsed with its own inner light. She claimed it without hesitation, drawn by its strange radiance as the portal continued to hum behind them, waiting.

They retreated to rest and plan their next move, but the gateway's call remained, promising answers and dangers in equal measure beyond the threshold of their world.
