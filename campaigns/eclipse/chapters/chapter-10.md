## The Brutal End

Ren suddenly twitched, stumbling as his hand gripped his head. He fell to one knee, breathing quickening before his head snapped up toward the Conductor. His steel mask fell to the ground with a metallic clang, revealing a wicked grin spreading across his face.

With inhuman speed, he rushed forward, his blade screaming through the wind as it sliced upward. The Conductor's arm was severed from her body in one clean cut. A second slash removed her bottom jaw, the bone and flesh dropping to the earth with a wet thud.

He looked at the severed arm on the ground, tilting his head before ripping the mask from her head and gripping her by the hair. He collected the severed limb, looking back at her with that same sinister grin. Pulling back her head, he began ramming the shoulder into her mouth, forcing it slowly down her throat. Gurgles, gagging, and choking were all that could be heard as the arm was forced down to elbow length.

Ren gripped both sides of her head, her remaining arm weakly beating against his side as he slowly pushed his thumbs into her eye sockets. His fingers held position as he pulled her head toward his knee, slamming the arm all the way down until only her hand hung from her mouth like some grotesque tongue.

He grinned, licking a canine before slowly pushing her onto her back. He stepped upon her head, chuckling softly as he spoke in barely a whisper, "Suffer.......slowly......you're but another message left in a gory bliss."

"Brutal.. Nice," Skyas commented as Ren finished his grisly work. "And to think she would've lived had she flirted back with D'vaari, what a shame." He coughed and took a deep breath. "That was fun for me personally, some of you looked like you were struggling though. The infirmary is gonna be suuuuper busy later."

Celeste stumbled forward as soon as it was clear the woman was dead, trying desperately to catch her breath through wheezing, wet coughs. She made it halfway to the taciturn Viera before giving up and half-collapsing to the ground. She caught a glimpse of the horror Ren had created and immediately stared at the ground, pretending it wasn't happening despite the sounds that would haunt her dreams.

Celeste reached out and clung to the Viera's clothes, clutching desperately and not letting go even as she sank to the ground. She tried to speak, but her first attempt caused another coughing fit. She locked eyes with them, then squeezed her own eyes shut, just trying to breathe.

The taciturn Viera looked decidedly away from Ren's brutality, falling into a brief coughing fit to clear the smog lingering in their lungs. They stumbled across the battlefield, light-headed and dizzy, stopping to catch the collapsing Celeste and block her vision from the bloody scene nearby.

"We survived," the taciturn Viera wheezed hoarsely while helping support Celeste. "Breathe and focus on that."

Celeste nodded, wheezing. "Breathing is... hard." She took several minutes trying to regain control. When she looked up, she saw Cassian staring at her as he walked past. "... I want to stand," she said quietly, slowly climbing to her feet with the Viera's help.

"Then try not to speak too much for now," the taciturn Viera advised. "We'll get the chance to rest soon. Until then we'll help you get to where you need."

Celeste nodded, still holding tightly onto the Viera's arm. She tried to lean around them to catch a glimpse of D'vaari, worry etched across her features. "You okay?" she whispered hoarsely to the Viera, looking them over.

The taciturn Viera kept their gaze averted from where the corpse had been, another brief coughing fit helping them avoid looking that direction. "We've been worse. Nothing some time in Bronze Lake won't be able to fix... We're glad we arrived in time to help. We worry what may have happened if we were too late."

As the Conductor died, the train terminal lay absolutely mangled and disabled, cutting off the region from resupply and leaving the Watcher's forces isolated. The toxic smog began to clear as her body vanished into anima, forming a red haze that drifted toward a large golden chest thrown from one of the trains. The chest's lid opened slightly, revealing a bright green glow from within. The anima faded away, leaving only the mysterious container and what appeared to be a green stone and a dull, faded cloak inside.

Ayre stepped toward the chest, seeing the cloak inside. She reached down and grasped it, lifting it up for all to see as she turned it over in her hands. "What in the name of gods is this?..."

The moment Ayre touched the cloak, an invisible force tore it from her grasp. It hung suspended in the air, casting projections on either side—a white cloak glowing with wisdom and light on one side, a black cloak radiating darkness and drinking in shadows on the other.

The moment V'ika moved near the floating cloak and brushed it slightly, there was an immediate reaction. A blinding white pulse exploded from the fabric, then it moved rapidly, hooking itself around V'ika's body. It settled over his shoulders, a long white hood coming to rest upon his head. He found himself fumbling and dropping his star globe. He clutched his head and fell to his knees, ears flattening as his tail twitched uncontrollably. 

V'ika squealed as the cloak forced itself upon him. He held his head, trying to make sense of the flood of memories—a date with his beloved, a wholesome moment with his child, then a spiteful man saying he would make him pay. "Wha- what in the seven hells!?" He was visibly shaking, not liking the situation or this strange cloak one bit.

V'ika slowly stood back up, groaning as he shook his head. "..where.. oh right.. hm.." He took out his PILs before walking toward the group, still confused and rubbing his temples.

Fiora ran to D'vaari as the Conductor fell, desperately pulling splints and bandages from her bag. "Don't move a muscle," she growled, her voice raw with exhaustion as she surveyed the damage from multiple train impacts. Her hands shook as she worked, applying pressure to the worst bleeding while blood seeped through her fingers.

She pushed the Dawnbringer's aether through her trembling palms, muttering through gritted teeth as fury and desperation colored her words. She was fighting to keep him alive against impossible odds.

D'vaari gave a small laugh and a thumbs up before falling back with a thump. He was completely spent.

Fiora yanked back her hood, gasping for air as sweat beaded on her forehead. She fumbled for another vial with blood-slicked fingers, downing its contents in one desperate gulp before turning back to D'vaari's mangled form, channeling every ounce of renewed energy into his worst injuries.

Rove collapsed onto the ground, breathing heavily and staring up at the crimson sky, truly wondering how he was still alive. Cassian stretched as he applied first aid to himself, looking around to make sure no more enemies would catch them off guard.

Ren walked over to check on D'vaari, tilting his head as he watched Fiora work. He knelt to collect his steel mask, now caked and stained with the Conductor's blood, before placing it back over his face. He looked at D'vaari with concern before moving to the back of the group.

V'ika moved toward D'vaari's prone form. "..What exactly happened with him and the.. trains." He was still rubbing his head from his earlier episode.

Fiora handed V'ika a roll of bandages. "Help me keep pressure. It's..." She shook her head, unable to finish the sentence. Rove quietly approached where Fiora worked, shuffling through his bag before setting down three Max-Potions. He walked back silently and sat against a wall, just watching. Fiora picked up the Max-Potions and tilted D'vaari's head up, forcing the healing liquid down his throat one bottle after another.

D'vaari coughed, sputtered, then drank.

Cassian offered a single nod to Celeste after making sure she was looking at him. "You did well. Good job." His gaze was no longer judging, but seemed to recognize her as part of something, maybe part of something he belonged to as well.

Ayre pressed a hand to her burned face, wincing at the contact. "Gotta say... for my first outing with you all.. this was quite the fight... Maybe that joke about you all being mercenaries ain't all laughs... Damn this wound smarts..."

The train terminal stood in ruins around them, smoke rising from twisted metal and shattered stone. But they had won. The Conductor was dead, the supply lines cut.
