The Phoenix Vanguard had secured Gloomport. With Cassian given time to complete whatever it was he had been drawing in his partial coma state, they boarded the only ship left at port and crossed the dark waters toward the Brood Pits, the alleged lair of whatever creature spawned the abominations that roamed this region.

The Watcher's soldiers made frequent visits to this place. They shepherded the beasts created here onto ships bound for the mainland. As the group neared the island, one such vessel had passed in the opposite direction, paying them no mind. Its crew was too occupied with keeping a large beast on deck under control to bother with them.

Bones lay scattered across the shore, intermingled with the broken masks of locals. On the far side of the island, dark red webs draped around the mouth of a large cave. Most of what could be explored here lay underground, behind that dark opening.

Fiora hung back at the rear of the group, her attention on Cassian rather than the shore. She did not trust that he was fully himself. "Better impressions could be made," she muttered, sizing up the cave entrance as the rest approached. She knelt and turned over one of the broken masks in her hands.

Skyas leaned his arm on the hilt of his katana, an excited smirk pulling at his face as he took in the bone-strewn shore. "A deep, dark cave on a remote island in a realm we aren't entirely familiar with... Is anyone else here as excited as I am? This place has fun written all over it. Makes me sad I missed the pre-game mission for this."

Xyla descended from the ship as he was speaking, frowning at the cave as she turned toward it fully. Her boots met the bone-littered earth and she pulled her bow from her back, holding it ready in one hand. She turned to face the group and made a point of looking at each of them in turn, Z'iro included, her brows drawn tight.

"Webs that size and glowing red should keep you all on your toes. No running ahead of the group and into the unknown as some usually do." Her gaze did not soften when it landed on Skyas. "I will take someone's leg out if anyone goes ahead or attempts to go alone."

Cassian had not moved his eyes from the cave mouth. He drew a folded parchment from his vest, the jagged but half-decent map he had sketched while in his trance, and held it out toward Xyla. "Quartermaster." The word came out flat. "Not sure where we should begin, but I'd assume we're in this small isle southwest of this map. Where should we go here though. Not sure."

Once the other ship was out of sight, V’nhea scrambled out onto the bowsprit and perched there like a gargoyle, hunched and still, his eyes fixed on the red webs at the cave's mouth. He took in the strands from a distance, head tilted, taking measure of them long before they were within reach.

"I know, right?" Siegrid’s gaze went bright as she turned toward Skyas with a grin. "Just think of the ruins that could be inside. The things we could learn about."

She bent and lifted a dragon-like skull from among the bones, working its lower jaw open and closed in one hand. "Hey Tenebris, look. It could be one of your cousins." The blade at her hip huffed but offered no answer. "Like normal, you're not fun. Fine." She set the skull back down. 

"Charming place." L'layla's muttered dryly. She flicked the brim of her hat back into place with a thumb. "You lot really know how to ask for help." A faint grin tugged at her lips as her gaze settled on the cave entrance.

V'ika said nothing. He drew his journal from his bag and began writing as the others spoke, his ears swiveling to follow each voice in turn.

Z'iro strolled off the ship behind the rest of the group, his eyes on the ground. His boot came down on a large old femur and snapped it loud in the still air. He glanced around, his gaze settling on the cave's entrance. "Well, best we put this beast to the sword then deal with its spawn in the future. If I were to judge by the departing ship we saw, they just made a run here. Maybe less creatures to contend with for now."

Xyla took Cassian's parchment, eyeing it over before walking it across to Z'iro to offer for his review as well. "I don't know where we should have started, but it seems we are here, so..." Her grip on the bow tightened as she looked back at the cave. "This seems to be the place we will be starting from. Not that I'm happy about it, given who we encountered back there."

Z'iro accepted the map with the faintest nod. He gave it a brief look and tucked it into his coat for copies to be made later.

"Learning..?" Skyas laced his hands behind his head, the smirk still in place. "We don't do that here, it sucks the fun right out of the adventure. I'm just here for a good fight." He turned toward Xyla, and as her last words about who they had encountered settled on him, his ears fell back against his head. "Where's the sense of adventure, Xy?"

Siegrid shook her head as she straightened. "Both go hand in hand. Every adventure is something to learn, it is just about what you choose to learn on it. You're more combat I take, while I want to know more of the past before me." She turned and began making a slow circuit of the cave entrance, her gaze running along the rocks and the webs.

Cassian scanned the dark mouth of the cave, his jaw tightening. "Where's the taciturn Viera when you need a scout?" he muttered, then looked back at the group. He turned to Xyla, his voice dropping lower. "How do we approach this? What's our formation?"

"I'll shoot you twice if you try running ahead, in that case. How's that for an adventure for you?" Xyla turned a wicked grin on Skyas before her focus returned to Cassian.

"I'm down for a duel whenever you are, Xy." Skyas threw a sly smirk her way. "It'll have to wait until we're done wiping the floor with whatever is in the cave though."

L'layla's ears twitched at the mention of a duel. Her attention slid from the cave entrance toward Skyas, the faint grin creeping wider, and a soft amused hum escaped her before she caught it.

Xyla laughed, the sound short. "If I let you close enough to land a hit..." She gave a small shrug, then turned her attention to Cassian. "Do you have suggestions?" She angled her head to one side. "Given how we've seen things come for that coin you carry, I don't think you should be upfront at first."

Fiora set the broken mask back on the ground and rose, her eyes narrowing on Cassian.

V'ika closed his journal and lowered himself to the ground. He drew his divine deck from its pouch and began to shuffle, seeking answers of some sort in the well-worn cards.

V’nhea swung down from his perch, landing with a thud and a soft grunt that escaped before he could swallow it. Old joints, old bones. He began to inch his way forward, getting a closer look at the webs strung across the cave's mouth.

"For such a dreary cave, there are some very beautiful flowers just outside of it," Naf mused with a chuckle and a nod. "Mayhap a way to draw people in?" He wandered over to the flowers near the cave's edge, closed his eyes, and breathed in deeply.

Fiora rose and crossed over to Naf, her hand snapping out to knock a deformed skull from his grip. Cobwebs draped across the bone had set into ridges, giving it an almost ethereal shape where they crusted over the eye sockets. "We don't know what creatures are lurking, large or small. Take care of what you pick up. There are too many crevasses in that."

Z'iro continued looking around idly, his gaze tracing the line of the shore and the broken landscape beyond.


## Into the Cave

Cassian shifted his gaze toward V’nhea. "V, are you willing and capable of scouting this out?" He gestured toward the dark cave mouth. "Go ahead and pick the path. We'll follow a few fulms back to stay out of your way. If you see anything, use the linkshell to call it in."

V'nhea nodded once and began picking his way up along the path to the mouth of the cave. Up close, the stringy webs looked more like viscera than spider-silk. At least he thought so. "I think it is wise that we don't do anything. Least we want to find out hastily." He continued forth, sweeping his gaze across the ground for anything that might yet be amiss. 

Cassian followed a few fulms behind, his stride slow and silent, his hand hovering near his axe. He glanced back at the group and gave a single nod for them to follow.

Naf looked up at Fiora with wide eyes. "Ah thank you, Mistress Fiora. I-it just looked so pretty. For a skull, that is." He grinned at her, dusting his hands off, his gaze flicking briefly back toward the “flowers” before he made himself look away.

V'ika drew a card from his deck and studied it for a moment before standing. "We have a high chance against whatever is in there."

The cave within held a faint red glow from crystallized anima embedded in the walls, but the light barely carried far enough to make out anything more than the nearest stone. Skyas was barely resisting the urge to call out a loud 'hello' just to hear the cave echo it back at him.

V'nhea continued down into the darkened depths, pausing every so often to listen and watch. A snail's pace, but haste makes waste. His cat-like eyes adjusted to the gloom as he picked his careful way along the floor, on and on, gaze sweeping.

A loud thrum came from somewhere deeper, as if a cord on an instrument had been plucked.

_Skitter skitter._

"Curious," V’nhea murmured.

V'ika reached up and pulled off his blindfold; what little sight it had ever offered him was useless in dark like this. Xyla looked around with the moon cat's advantage of seeing past it and frowned softly, her bow held ready in one hand. Beside her, Naf drew out his portable lamp, filled it with oil, and lit it.

"Eh, is it?" D'vaari stretched as he followed along with the group. "Far as I can tell, this is just the usual shit this realm does. I'll give it another bell or so before we're jumped."

V’nhea stopped and looked back at the group. "Now, I don't want to alarm anyone. But." He stooped down and pointed carefully for those following. "We have tripwires. In the veritable belly of the beast, as it were."

"Tripwires?" L'layla muttered. "Wonderful. Nothing says welcoming cave like traps inside a monster nest."

Skyas could not see too well inside the cave, but he listened out for anything moving that wasn't the group. V’nhea slowly pinched his fingers together in the air, asking for quiet. "I'd also advise lowered voices. We don't know what is in here. This doesn't look man made."

Cassian moved in a semi-crouch, his hand hovering near his weapon. He squinted, forcing his vision to adjust to the poorly illuminated cave. "Traps. Whatever it triggers could give out our location. Probably easier to avoid it."

L'layla gave a small shrug.

V’nhea rose and looked further beyond before stepping over the strand. "Thing is, if this has any indication to what I think it does, the same stuff outside is there for the same reason."

Z'iro stepped over the wire once V’nhea had pointed it out, casting a glance back toward the group before returning his gaze forward.

"Hmm." Xyla's gaze ran the length of the cavern walls. "There's also a plethora of tunnels throughout this cave. I wager if those things I just caught watching us and darting away across the ceiling are anything to go by..." She frowned at the group. "Well, they know we are here at least, whatever it is. Let's be mindful of the traps though, eh?"

Siegrid looked about, her gaze flicking over the rocks for anything beyond what was directly ahead of them. Naf raised the lamp a little higher, trying to illuminate as much as he could, hoping to catch any signs of traps near the group.

D'vaari reached up and slowly untied the eyepatch about his face, revealing the Pomander of Sight he had shoved into the socket of the missing eye. "Like I said, it's just a matter of time before things drop on us. It's the way things tend to happen here regardless of how we proceed. If there's something inside? It'll rush us as soon as we get deep enough inside that we can't make a hasty retreat without it getting the time to maul a few of us."

V'ika moved closer to the group, his gaze fixed ahead in the hope of catching the first sign of whatever was moving in the dark.

"So then, what?" V’nhea asked, gesturing at the wire. "Spring the trap?"

Cassian stepped cleanly over the tripwire, adjusting his stride without breaking rhythm.

"More that we should leave it," D'vaari said. "And if we proceed, utilize it as part of said retreat to try and lure things into them."

"Again, this trip wire is not man made." V’nhea's gaze flicked back to the strand. "The same stuff as what is on the entrance."

"No, we aren't springing any traps right now." Xyla's frown deepened. "...and if any of you do, I _will_ do that bootcamp I mentioned before we came across here. And I'll have Z'iro throw in a trip up the Kugane tower for the hell of it."

Skyas's expression went grim as his eyes followed the direction of the sounds. He kept his voice low, low enough that nothing outside the Vanguard could pick it up. "Guys, we aren't alone. Whatever the creatures that are in here with us, they're intelligent enough to communicate with each other. Worryingly so. They have speech patterns and everything. It's too formal for these beasts to be considered mindless."

Fiora brought up the rear, Dawnbringer gripped tight in one hand. Her other drifted toward the vials at her side, then stopped halfway. She made a fist and let it fall back, the sigh that escaped her short and quiet.

D'vaari summoned his mask into his hand, the smoke of summoning curling away as he turned it in his grip and looked it square in the eye sockets. "What do you think, Screamer? Think they would want to talk to you?" He held the mask up to his ear and listened. "Yeah, nah. Probably not."

V'ika's tail puffed slightly. "We were being watched. A large creature for sure, with possibly ten glowing eyes. It went deeper into a tunnel. If I could give an idea... spiders of some sort?" He pointed in the direction he had seen it.

Cassian glanced down at the strand and then back into the darkness. "Whatever it is, it is territorial. Perhaps protecting the nest of whatever it is they are brooding." His voice stayed low. "We should keep moving. We need to get to the heart of it."

"Well, we could confuse the wires, possibly." V’nhea gave a small shrug and started forward again.

L'layla's nose wrinkled immediately. "Ten glowing eyes?" she whispered. "Fantastic. I already hated this cave." Her hand settled firmly on the grip of her rapier as she looked toward the tunnel V'ika had indicated. "If something with that many eyes starts crawling at me, I'm stabbing first and panicking second."

_Skitter skitter._

"It was large, if that helps, L'layla," V'ika offered.

_Click click click._

"Well, if they didn't know we were here, they do now," V’nhea said.

"I haven't seen a damn thing," Cassian muttered.

"Please stop talking, V'ika," L'layla said.

V’nhea scanned the room. "Neither have I. And that's the problem."

V'ika met L'layla's look with an innocent smile.

The group continued moving through the cave as they spoke.

"Alright, I'll start then," D'vaari said. "I bet the mandibles are huge."

"Something tells me the large one Vika was talking about went to warn the others," Skyas said. "Assuming that _wasn't_ the big guy himself we came to kill. We'll have to tear the whole cave down to kill them all at this rate."

The cave brightened slightly down to the left. A shadow darted across the path ahead, about the size of a small dog, its limbs producing a sharp skittering sound as they moved. It vanished down a small passage at floor level.

"They scurried down the tunnels above or out of sight," Xyla said, "and if what Skyas heard is true, they likely have a plan. Given the amount of masks out front, is it possible that whatever it is consumed villagers and took on their..." She paused, her gaze drifting upward, one ear flicking as she listened.

Naf shivered at the clicking echoing around the chamber. "Gods, but that clicking is freaking me out." His free hand fidgeted, itching for his bow.

Siegrid kept her hand at the ready on Tenebris. "Oh see, he's just a little guy." She smiled. "It's not that bad."

"We really got to desensitize you newcomers," D'vaari said.

Cassian's hand tightened on his hilt as the skittering came and went. "Careful with those small dog-sized things," he warned, his voice low.

V'ika watched the shadow dart off, listening for any further sign of the creature. "That's one little guy. I know what I saw."

"Well, there are certainly plenty of the smaller ones." Xyla's frown returned. "I have a feeling I should have brought more of a certain something..."

V’nhea slowly drew his blades. "Well." He pointed. "Ankle biters." He continued picking his way along the floor, pausing from time to time to peer up at the ceiling and then back down to the floor.

"Now's as good a time as any." Skyas turned to D'vaari. "They're here, aren't they? Even if they didn't sign up for highly intelligent spider-like creatures, that's what they're getting. Too bad I left the bug spray back at the manor." He gave a dramatic shrug.

"You know..." D'vaari mused. "I wonder if they can talk. That'd be neat. I'd love to talk to 'em for a little bit. Always interesting to see new fauna."

Only one way deeper ran ahead of them now. The cavern brightened significantly down the path, a vibrant red glow spilling out from around the corner. The walls leading in were cast in shadow, but they seemed to be moving. A faint grinding and skittering noise filled the space the group now stood in. Thick red webs covered the walls and hung from the ceiling. Every now and then a large shadowy mass skittered along the stone, further into the cavern.

Naf breathed a little easier, managing a chuckle at Skyas's levity. "Aye, I just hope they aren't too intelligent..." His smile turned grim by the end.

"They can," Skyas answered, "though I'd bet my next payday they're more interested in tearing us apart than spilling their secrets." He looked back toward the faint skittering. "They were speaking to each other earlier..." His gaze drifted up to the ceiling once he had taken in the rest of the environment.

"Language, or just click clack we're arachnids?" D'vaari asked.

"It was too faint to make out, and it wasn't any language I know," Skyas said. "But they had speech patterns. Certain sounds in different tones."

Cassian's hand gripped his hilt tighter as he tracked the shifting shadows. "Keep your blades ready and stay centered. Watch the shadows," he muttered to the group.

"What if we don't have a sword?" D'vaari asked.

Cassian looked at D'vaari. "I don't think you need one."

"But please, Mr. Cassian, I'm just an innocent, pacifistic sailor~." D'vaari chuckled, then glanced around to the others. "Well... if they're intelligent enough, we might be able to persuade them otherwise. Why fight when we can gather an ally? But if you're all going in with the violent option, suppose there's not much to do about that one..."

L'layla gave a quiet hum in acknowledgment, sliding her rapier free with a soft hiss of steel. V'ika reached for his globe at his back, settling it into his hand and readying himself for the fight that was likely coming. Naf tied his lamp around his waist and brought out his bow and quiver in turn.

"You got it, boss," Naf said.

V’nhea lowered himself toward the floor and lifted his hand to signal a halt. "Alright... I think I see it." He watched the ceiling. "Those." He pointed. "Seem to be taking food in. And the others are going out to gather." He looked back. "Mind your hands, lest you find yourself coated in the smaller ones." He gestured to the walls.

Fiora glanced behind her, back toward the entrance. "Hopefully they don't come from behind."

Skyas unlatched his scabbard from his belt with his left hand while his right stayed tensed in case he needed to quickly draw his blade. He took note of the small spiders threaded through the webs that coated the walls of the inner part of the cave. "Didn't need to tell me twice. The second I figured out these little guys could talk, you couldn't pay me to drop my guard."

Z'iro took in the information, then nodded for V’nhea to continue when he was prepared.

"They seem to have that red stuff on their bodies, like blisters. At least, that's how they look." V’nhea rose and pressed on.

Cassian glanced back over his shoulder. "Fiora, get into formation next to V'ika."

Fiora shook her head at him, keeping her eyes on his movements in the dark as best she could. Cassian grunted and turned to face ahead again.

They reached a cavern lined with red crystals.

Naf took in V's information as they walked. "Food, huh. They wouldn't happen to be fruit eaters by any chance? Or would that be hoping for too much?" He eyed the walls as they moved.

"If this is anything like the last thing we faced, remember, names hold power in this land." Xyla spoke low, her gaze ahead and on the walls.

"I thought we already ruled that out with Bloodscreamer? Did it come back up again?" D'vaari's head tilted. "If so, I fucking knew it. I said that at the beginning."

"We didn't rule it out. The thing said as much to us, or indicated it. I am just reminding or informing those here."

L'layla glanced toward D'vaari with a slight frown. "...I'm sorry, a _what_ screamer?"

"It's this big bastard we fought around the first time we got in here," Skyas said, drawing out the word to emphasize just how big it was. "It's in Fiora's mission debrief files if you're curious after this."

"Gods dammit, months of progress on a hypothesis out the window only for it to come back up." D'vaari glanced over and held up his mask. "Oh, my little friend here. Say hello." He waved the blood red mask back and forth with a chuckle before letting out a long sigh. "Poor bastard, just misunderstood really. And now I have to start thinking about the implications again."


## Den of the Brood Sovereign

As the group made their way down the cavern's pathway into the final chamber, they were greeted with a horrific sight. The room was covered in eggs, both broken and unhatched, along the walls and across the ceiling. Within the translucent shells, spiders and other gruesome horrors grew in various shapes and sizes. Spiderlings dragged paralyzed civilians up to the eggs and stuffed them inside, where the things growing within set upon them.

The room stank of death, the air heavy with moisture that clung to skin. Perched at the center was a massive anima-warped spider, its hide bristling with razor-sharp spikes. A huge ovipositor connected to its rear abdomen pulsed steadily, producing fresh eggs which the worker spiders gathered and stuck to the quickly diminishing open spaces. These were spiders, certainly, but they moved like a nest of ants, efficient and highly protective of their domain.

The massive beast was turned sideways to the group. They had not fully alerted it. Yet.

Fiora gestured for silence and slowly backed further from the chamber. Her head twisted to watch the path behind her, looking for any nook from which to observe without being noticed. She moved quickly away from the wall as every cranny she eyed proved to be lined with eggs.

"Well... a well-aimed Garlean cocktail could do the trick, I think," V’nhea whispered.

"...Gods." All humor had drained from L'layla's voice. "That is the ugliest thing I've ever seen."

"I suggest a plan is made, and fast. Thoughts?" Xyla looked between Cassian and Z'iro. "I'll be taking my usual place at least." A grin pulled at her mouth as her gaze flicked upward, searching for higher ground.

"Now that's one ugly fuck. Kids are even worse to look at but maybe that's just ‘cause I hate spiders...." Skyas kept his voice to a low whisper. "If no one else is volunteering to get the party started I'm more than happy to step forward. It's like a public service, removing one less species of spider from the world."

The Brood Sovereign bristled at the sound of voices.

Naf's eyes widened at the gruesome sight, his gaze pulled to the paralyzed civilians being fed to the hatchlings. He took a step forward without realizing. "We have to help them," he whispered, anger rising in his voice as he pulled an arrow from his quiver. "They're still alive." He glanced at the group, then turned to look the looming beast over for weak points.

V’nhea slid behind one of the larger eggs to break line of sight, his footsteps careful to not disturb the dirt as he went. Cassian's jaw set as he took in the scene. He had not wasted a movement; his axe was already in hand. "There's the source." The whisper came clipped. His eyes did not leave the giant horror at the center. "It's a hive. We could hit the big one hard and fast before the rest of the nest swarmed. If only we had some explosives..." His gaze cut to Xyla as he spoke.

D'vaari reached up to his Pomander, channeling aether into it and through his body as he crouched ever so slightly. A pulse of aether went out from the device. "Don't care about the civilians, let ‘em die, might provide a decent distraction."

Siegrid stared at the beast with a hunger of her own, the sword in her hand glowing with void light in anticipation. _Finally a meal,_ the blade said. She nodded along. "I know that right, it should settle your needs for a good bit if it doesn't kill us."

"All I can say is, have hope in the stars. They will guide us to victory." V'ika looked toward D'vaari. "Perhaps we get a bonus of some sort if they're alive however." A frown settled across his face.

"Then let the Twelve sort them out." With that, D'vaari shot forward in a zig-zag, a blur of lightning straight into the den of the beast.

V’nhea started poking the egg he was standing behind with the pointy end of his sword.

"If they're well fed, they'll be stronger. The civilians are not our priority, but we should keep them from a meal if we can," Fiora whispered.

Siegrid flashed her blade and ran out after D'vaari. "Right then, it's meal time. Let's hope it's a spicy one."

"Choice has been made. Tanks up front, healers and range in the back, the rest where you prefer." Xyla's voice cut clean across the chamber. "_Move_ before they attack first."

The Brood Sovereign whirled to face D'vaari and the rest of the group with a shudder of its bulk. The workers and soldiers along the walls shrieked in alarm, the whole hive snapping into motion at once.

"If you can burn them, do it. Do not let them swarm you." Cassian's tone stayed low even as the call carried, his eyes locked on Fiora a heartbeat longer than the rest. Then he turned and walked ahead. "And do not fall too far behind."

"Less food for them to consume the better," V'ika said, louder this time so all could hear.

Naf nodded grimly at Xyla, taking up position next to her, nocking and drawing an arrow.

"Well, if D'vaari doesn't die in the fight, it'll be fun to see Xyla shoot him with an arrow." Skyas leapt into battle not far behind the others.

L'layla blinked once as D'vaari launched himself into the nest. "...Oh, we're doing this immediately then." A sharp grin returned despite the horror around her as she drew her rapier fully and dashed after him, boots splashing through the damp cavern floor. "Try not to die before I get my turn!"

Behind them, spiderlings sealed off the cave entrance, weaving it shut with tight anima webs.


## Enmeshed in the Web

Xyla found high ground that was clear and pulled back on her bow. An arrow appeared along the string, shimmering with stars down the shaft. A glimmer of light joined it as it zipped past.

The ovipositor at the Brood Sovereign's abdomen pulsed and deposited two very large eggs onto the stone. The shells split open as they landed, and from them ripped two massive armored spiders, eight legs each tipped in sickle-like blades, darting forward to join their queen.

D'vaari did not slow. He slammed his weight into the first of the two guards, shoulder-checking the creature away from the queen with a feral hiss, his eyes going wide as the pupil narrowed to a slit. As the guardian began to recover, he drew a deep breath and roared, the sound akin to a Behemoth's. His fists slammed into the hardened frontal carapace, driving cracks along the seams of its exoskeleton, leaving vulnerabilities for those who would follow.

Skyas charged the other Praetorian, unsheathing his blade in a quickdraw strike that slammed down onto the guardian's front leg, while Naf released his arrow at the same target, fiery petals trailing in its wake as it found the mark. The sound of clocks ticked through the chamber for a moment, and a blue star flew out of V'ika's globe to strike one of the newly-born guards. Fiora drew aether from Dawnbringer and pulled it together in a dense weave; it launched toward the same guardian, wrapped around its legs, and solidified into rock before crumbling.

V’nhea raced forward to close the distance. One might have expected him to attack a praetorian. He did not. Instead, he leapt onto the thorax of one and used it as a springboard, both blades sweeping around to land a double slice across the ovipositor at the Brood Sovereign's rear.

Siegrid ran up with the blade in both hands, knowing her place among the more heavily armored. The dice set into Tenebris's hilt rolled, and the head of a flame dragon erupted along the steel and coated it in fire. She swung down at the Brood Sovereign in a heavy arc. "Oi, you're going to make a fine meal. How's the taste, Tenebris?"

L'layla rushed the right-side guardian and thrust her blade into its eye.

The Brood Sovereign shrieked in anger as its guardians fell almost as fast as they had risen. It swiveled its massive body toward D'vaari, its maw falling open, and unleashed a blast of searing hot anima. The wave caught D'vaari head-on, and Skyas and Siegrid took it as collateral damage. The anima clung to them for a few long moments, burning and searing into armor and exposed skin before mercifully fading. A massive leg followed, swung down at D'vaari, smashing into the ground inches from him.

D'vaari reached and tore one of the mandibles from the fallen Praetorian, holding it high. "One!" he cried, only to be blasted by the flame. When the wave passed, he stood there a moment, aether rippling about his person as water steamed off of him. "Bitch." His gaze found the queen. Then a leg slammed down beside him. "And you're a clutz." He reared a fist back and cracked it across the leg with such force the heavy plating indented, his body's strength in overdrive with aether.

V’nhea continued his onslaught on the ovipositor a moment longer, then his gaze flicked up to the glowing joints of the beast's legs. _Shadow of the Colossus._ He leapt up from the egg-laying sack and slammed his blades into what looked to be a weak point at the joint.

Fiora had tried to throw a shield of condensed aether around D'vaari as the Brood Sovereign's maw opened, but she could only watch as the anima washed over him. She gritted her teeth and twisted the aether out of its intended shape, letting it fall over D'vaari and the others to soothe their burns.

Cassian lunged in a low swing, his axe cutting through the armored leg of the Brood Sovereign and leaving a clean gash, then kept one eye on the back line to see if he needed to swing back.

Soulblossom crackled with Skyas's aetheric lightning, charging for a massive strike. The blade even started to glow for a moment, then stopped just short of activating on its own.
