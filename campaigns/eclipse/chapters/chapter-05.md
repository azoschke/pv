The sun hung high in a cloudless sky as the Phoenix Vanguard emerged from the portal, trading the eternal crimson twilight of the realm of eclipse for the brilliant warmth of their island base. Salt-tinged wind carried the scent of newly cut timber and fresh construction as they made their way along the path. Z'iro's investment during their absence stretched before them - an entire village rising from what had been an empty coastline, its buildings far more elaborate than necessity demanded.

"Starving," the taciturn Viera muttered, their stomach growling audibly as they followed behind their captain.

Rove Fendewood, the diminutive Lalafell who had somehow attached himself to their recent expedition, lingered at the rear of the group. His small frame barely cast a shadow on the sun-warmed path, and he seemed uncertain whether the others had even noticed his presence.

Xyla walked alongside Z'iro, her eyes constantly scanning their surroundings despite the relative safety of their own territory. Her shoulders rolled slightly as she worked out the tension from their recent battles. Behind them, Visaki voiced the question that had been nagging at him since Z'iro's earlier inspection of the group.

"Do you think we're impostors? With respect, of course."

Z'iro's only response was a knowing huff of laughter as he surveyed his expensive gambit. The buildings were not merely functional but architecturally impressive, with proper foundations and quality materials throughout. "Well, this is an improvement," he declared, taking a long draw from his smoke. "It'll be nice to be able to sleep in a proper bed tonight, get some real food."

As they reached the village's center, Fiora slumped against a post, exhaustion written in every line of her body. She kicked off her sandals and dug her toes into the warm earth, seeking comfort in the familiar sensation after days in that aether-starved realm. Her hands moved automatically, scooping up a handful of dirt.

Fiora finished her dirt-gathering and began ascending the stairs, her gathered earth carefully cupped in one hand. As she climbed, she passed Rove, her gaze clearer than their last encounter. She stared at him for a moment before shaking her head and continuing upward. Rove, believing himself potentially invisible, gave Fiora a cheeky mischievous grin and put a finger to his lips, making a silent 'shhh' before she walked past.

The main group ascended to the rooftop terrace of the largest building, while Rove lingered on the stairs, watching nervously. Z'iro seated himself at the terrace table, looking around with evident satisfaction. "Well, I think this was a fantastic purchase, an investment into our success. Speaking of - someone go get that short little man on the stairs with the hat, I'd like to have a word with that one."

The taciturn Viera posted themselves against the gazebo wall, pulling trail rations from their pack to begin munching quietly. Visaki settled in along the wall, his shoulders finally dropping as tension melted away. Xyla eyed the small village with narrowed eyes before raising a questioning eyebrow at Z'iro, her silent inquiry about the cost receiving only a shrug in response.

When no one moved to fetch the Lalafell, Z'iro sighed and descended himself. His boot nudged Rove, whose face instantly filled with panic.

"Oh! Hello there good sir! I just happened to be taking a small breather here, I should probably best be on my way!" He said and started to turn to run down the stairs.

Z'iro stared down at him for a moment before leaning down, grabbing him by the back of his shirt. "I think not," he said nonchalantly with a small hint of amusement.

"I DIDN'T HEAR ANYTHING I SWEAR I WON'T TELL A SOUL ANYTHING JUST DON'T KILL MEEEEE," Rove wailed as he was carried up the stairs.

"Ah, Finally got him to come out of hiding then?" Xyla smiled playfully as she eyed the captive Lalafell.

The taciturn Viera stepped from the wall as Z'iro returned, beginning to pull their lance from their back as they asked, "Do you want us to end them?"

"I WARN YOU I AM VERY DANGEROUS! MANY PEOPLE WILL COME SEARCHING FOR ME IF I GO MISSING," Rove lied desperately.

Z'iro held Rove above one of the free chairs and opened his hand, letting the Lalafell unceremoniously fall into the cushion. "You're in it now friends, take a seat." He returned to his previous seat, flopping back into it as he looked to the taciturn Viera. "He'll be alright, his been conscripted into the cause. Welcome to the crew." He looked back to Rove. "Name?"

Meanwhile, Fiora had taken a seat and begun kneading the dirt into a cylindrical form. At the word "conscription," the dirt crumbled in her hands, and she ground her teeth before taking a deep breath and meticulously rebuilding the form.

"Apologies... I do get frightened easily when prevented from escaping as I feel anyone would. I am Rove, Rove Fendewood. And uhm... what do you mean by conscripted...?" Rove asked, regaining his composure.

Z'iro slowly arched a brow, staring at Rove. "Well, Rove. You're one of two things now - a mercenary for hire, or a pirate - or both. If nothing else, you'll have plenty of new things to stuff into your bag." He turned his gaze from Rove, reaching into his coat to place the map on the table. "Our hosts were kind enough to draw this up for us, it might be useful moving forward."

"Now, I figure we review it together before I release you all to some relaxation and socialization - Oh, and for your pleasure I also had them install a hot tub in this place, feel free to use it. You'll find it on the cliff edge behind me."

"Oh I'm sorry, I think there's been some kind of misunderstanding, I'm no fighter, I'm a tradesman!" Rove said with a slight chuckle as if such information would change his circumstance.

"A.... Hot Tub? H- You know what, I'll find out soon enough." Xyla sighed, shaking her head.

Distracted now, Fiora placed the dirt back in a jar in her bag. Cassian had been keeping to himself, gazing around the room. He leaned back and crossed his arms in a mildly relaxed posture, shooting small glances at Fiora from time to time.

Z'iro laughed. "Yes, a hot tub - and a lighthouse, and a few other.. important things." He looked back to Rove. "Good, we'll make use of those skills, however it leaves your situation unchanged. You should count yourself lucky, we also own two houses and a ship - all with rooms for crew members. You can sell your wares as we travel port to port and I won't even charge you for the cargo. Now then - the map."

"Oh yes, All essential elements for a place that once we are done will likely never come back too." Xyla rolled her eyes, shaking her head as she continued to lean back in her chair, arms still folded as she finally eyed over the map.

Fiora held up a hand to Cassian, wiggling it. Cassian took her hand, helping her up with ease.

The taciturn Viera spoke up. "Seems like good time to discuss all that has happened so far, but..." They paused, glancing about the members present. "Only few of us here..."

As Cassian took Fiora's hand, she signed something to him before attempting to get a better look at the map. Cassian shot a glance at Fiora, giving her a small nod as he also tried to take a better look at the map with a curious expression.

"That's true, only a few of us here. I'm hoping they just couldn't make it," Visaki agreed.

Z'iro unrolled the map on the table for everyone to see. It was crude, likely drawn quickly over the span of a day or so. It showed the village where they'd first visited, the realm gates, and seven branching paths leading deeper into the realm of the eclipse. Each path sported towns, villages, and cities, with names at the end: The Watcher, The Executioner, The Hand, The Bishop, The Consort, The Crimson King, and one partially erased reading "Th- E--led Q----."

"We have a larger problem than where to go," Fiora grumbled, keeping her voice low.

Z'iro reached up, scratching his cheek in thought as he glanced down at the map. "Now clearly, it would be a bad sort of idea to expect we could confront this Crimson King immediately, so we should start down one of the other paths, those with a name would be smartest. Does anyone have a preference? We do know that when we were confronting Ironwind, The Collector mentioned he was an agent of 'The Watcher', that may be a start."

Rove nodded along, pretending to know what this all meant, sure he'd get an actual rundown later and resorting to simply taking notes.

Z'iro looked over to Fiora, arching a brow. "Speak your mind."

Fiora let out a heavy sigh, before straightening her posture. She addressed Z'iro with a clipped, formal tone, "Sir, the ambient aether is almost nonexistent. Our chances of survival are slim if we keep going as we are." Her voice cracked before she masked it, "I am not strong enough to handle it."

"The hand or The Bishop could be an idea also, given that I suspect they'd at least have information, although, Given the name the watcher has, it might be ideal to head his direction and reclaim any of -our- information he has to keep them from knowing so much," Xyla suggested.

The taciturn Viera stepped forward and, after reviewing the map, reached out to point a finger to the Consort's path.

Cassian patted his front pocket, taking the sigil out and examining it for a moment with intense focus before putting it back. His face remained expressionless as he continued listening to the group.

Z'iro leaned back in his chair, considering Fiora's observation. "Indeed, it is a problem. However, to say you are not strong enough - I disagree. I doubt anyone here would argue that the key to our survival thus far in battle aside from each of our individual skills, has been you. Do you have any ideas how we might resolve this issue? If not, this may be a discussion for you and the Guardian back in the Gardens of Eternity. Take Cassian with you."

There was slight hesitation in Fiora's reply, "The Gardens of Eternity, sir?"

Cassian tilted his head, continuing to listen.

"You forget Z'iro, most of those with us don't know about what came before. Perhaps clueing them in might be an idea," Xyla reminded him.

"Where Hearthorn found us," the taciturn Viera called, thumbing back to the faintly glowing lance at their back.

Z'iro smiled. "The place where each of us found our weaponry you've seen in battle. Taci's spear, Xyla's bow, my swords, Skyas sword, Joundi's sword, The woman's staff, C'Verna's sword and shield. Perhaps there, you two might find the means to further aid the group. It very well may be that our success rests on what you find there." He continued, "Of course, we'll bring you two to the island but your path is your own once you get there - it is not ours to walk until you return to the beach where we'll be waiting."

"I agree Fiora's stronger than most would realize, but even the strongest foundations can crack if too much weight is left on them. If there's something this Guardian can do to help... then yeah. I'll go with her," Cassian stated.

Fiora offered a succinct nod, the trepidation in her expression quickly masked. "Yes, sir."

"The weapons were quite impressive when I saw them. If I get the chance to wield such a weapon, I'll take it as well," Visaki commented.

Z'iro nodded to Visaki, then looked to Rove. "You two should go as well then they're done, the Garden's of Eternity are.. unique, there is no true way to describe the place, you'll have to see it for yourselves. We'll drop you at the beach, when each of you decides to venture inland but once you do, you walk that path without the rest of the crew. I've never seen more than two individuals enter at the same time."

Z'iro turned his head back just enough to see Vika. "You included."

Vika's ears perked up, seeming to have zoned out. "Oh.. uh.. Okay?"

"Challenge accepted," Visaki said simply.

"Honestly Fiora, you do not give yourself enough credit, you joined us and threw yourself into all of this immediately and I can say, without a doubt, given certain peoples absences and being A.W.O.L that we all wouldn't be alive right now if you hadn't have been here and therefore we wouldn't have been able to make as much progress, so -please- stop running yourself down or doubting yourself," Xyla insisted.

Fiora placed her palms flat against her thighs, leaning heavily on the post. "If the aether is not there," she trailed off, swallowing. "What happens if there is none left?"

Z'iro returned his attention to the map. "Now then, we have two votes to chase down 'The Watcher' and one vote for 'The Consort'. Does anyone else wish to cast a vote?"

Cassian looked at Xyla and let out a quiet breath, "She deserves the credit. Every word of that's true, but that doesn't mean she's invincible." He looked at Fiora with quiet worry. "She'll keep going until something breaks. And that's not allowed to happen."

Fiora's voice turned flinty again, pushing down the emotion, "How much should we trust these titles? The watcher...watches? Informs?"

Cassian glanced back at Z'iro. "I vote for The Watcher too. Securing communications, intel... it's worth more than just another body down. Could shift the whole table if we know what they know."

Z'iro nodded to Fiora. "As much as we can trust those in the village I suppose. We have no other leads at the moment other than to start down the path and see where it leads us. I'm sure if we find the challenge too much for the moment we can always turn back and try another path, make progress where and when we can. We'll absolutely need to make smart decisions, however. I don't think D'Vaari's change in head first strategy will remain viable forever."

"Cassian's logic is sound, I'll vote for the Watcher." Visaki thought for a moment. "D'varri's strategy could've been deadly last time, it's true...."

"We need to learn what we're up against. There are more questions than answers," Fiora said, her eyes fixed on the map.

Rove seemed to have zoned out but realized he was spoken to about the Gardens of Eternity. "I fail to see how such a thing would assist me.. as I said earlier I am no fighter."

"Then make self useful. Otherwise die," the taciturn Viera stated bluntly.

Vika mumbled, "Really.. interesting cast of characters.."

"I-I never said I lacked use! I simply stated that weaponry seems wasted on one such as myself!" Rove protested.

"I agree," Cassian said in a firm tone. "We need to start approaching things with more strategy, especially moving forward. Charging in headfirst might work once or twice, but eventually we're going to hit something that won't be impressed by boldness." He folded his arms.

Z'iro glanced back at Vika. "You've heard the conversation thus far, what's your input on all of this? I assume you'll be joining us, we could certainly use the extra pair of hands, it's been rough going thus far despite us getting the job done."

"I uh.. well I tried to.. but.. I can try to help as long as my memory doesn't fail me like always.." Vika replied.

Cassian nudged Fiora and made a few hand signs while glancing over in Vika's direction.

"Hard to know yet," Fiora muttered in response.

Z'iro chuckled lightly, giving a nod. "Good enough, we'll see what you're made of before long I suspect." He looked back at the group. "Then it's settled, we'll begin down 'The Watcher's' path for now and see what we can uncover. I'll see about getting us a forward base setup at the Realm Gates in the realm of the eclipse itself, though we'll keep this place maintained so we can recover in more peace than we'll ever find there. The longer we stay there without a break, the more that place will wear us down by pure attrition."

The dark circles under Fiora's eyes reflected her exhaustion, as she brought one hand up to massage her sternum. Small tremors shook her hand, as much as she attempted to disguise it.

"Should stock on ether for journeys inside," the taciturn Viera suggested.

"It can be addictive," Fiora warned.

Z'iro rolled the map up, tucking it back into his jacket. "Tomorrow, we'll set sail for the Garden's of Eternity, Fiora and Cassian will head inland to find the means to assist us further. At a later date, we'll return for Visaki, Rove and our quiet friend behind me."

It was then that Steven came diving into the room, slamming into the table before picking himself up and letting out a rather loud, obnoxious seagull noise toward Z'iro, who paid him no attention.

"Addictive--but if faced with emergency, would be useful to carry," the taciturn Viera responded.

The bird's antics pulled Fiora from her dark musings as she snorted.

Vika silently took out his journal and began writing in it, trying to keep track of what was said and hopefully have most of it down before looking up confused by the bird.

"OH GOD IT'S DEAD! IT'S DEAD! DEAR LORD IN HEA- oh..." Rove threw his hands up almost comically as the bird slammed into the table.

Xyla watched Steven and laughed. "Nice of you to join us finally." She smiled slightly and looked around the group. "I think we could all use a good rest, some more than others and I suggest more than a day or two before we head back in there." Her gaze landed on Z'iro as she nodded slightly.

"I'm interested in this hot tub honestly...." Visaki said.

Steven walked across the table, carrying on loudly before finally getting Z'iro's attention. The captain snatched him off the table and stuffed him into his jacket pocket head first. Half of the abnormally large bird's back end hung out, his legs kicking wildly through his loud seagull noises, now significantly muted by the fabric.

"Well, until tomorrow, you're all welcome to rest here and make use of the facilities. Don't spill anything in the hot tub, it was expensive," Z'iro said.

Cassian looked over at the group, then at Fiora. "If it ever looks like you're pushing yourself to the point it's going to kill you," he said plainly, "I'm not waiting on a vote or an order. I'll carry you out of there myself."

"Understood. I'll come in sword-free. Who knows what would happen if I brought them in, some sort of aetheric reaction maybe," Visaki replied to Z'iro, giving a sort of salute gesture.

"I don't think I've ever used a hot tub..." Cassian pondered.

"I'd rather rest, and I don't want my things left unsupervised, mm?" Fiora elbowed Cassian.

"My comment was not meant as I want to use the hot tub, it was more of just stating facts," Cassian clarified.

Z'iro stood, smoothing out his jacket, Steven still flailing in his pocket. "There are rooms for each of you, if you decide to share then just.. keep it to a reasonable volume. I'll be down at the ship in my cabin for the evening, I have some work to finish." He looked to Xyla, then to the rest of the group. "Tomorrow for two of you, the rest of you soon and we're back into the realm of the eclipse next week."

Xyla raised an eyebrow as he looked at her, turning her gaze to the horizon.

Vika just had zero clue what he was in for and just hoped his memories didn't cause an absolute disaster.
