## The Plan

The group had pushed south from Valorspire, their only safe foothold within The Watcher's region, moving toward Gloomport with the hope of securing a vessel. The black lake lay beyond the port, and beyond that, the Brood Pits—where they could put an end to whatever was spawning the monstrosities that wandered the dead woods, feeding on locals and stalking their every step. The crimson moon hung full overhead as it had since their arrival, fixed and unblinking.

As they neared the outskirts of Gloomport, the weight of anima in the air thickened until it sat heavy in their lungs, far worse than anywhere else they'd been. A presence perhaps, or something worse. From the ridge, the port town spread below them, crawling with soldiers of the Crimson Court moving in large patrols through the streets. A staging area. On the far side of town sat a ship of some size, likely used to transport creatures from the Brood Pits to the mainland.

Fiora leaned against Cassian, one hand pressed to her side, jaw tight though she tried to keep the evidence of the strain off her face. Cassian kept an arm close, his gaze sweeping the terrain ahead even as he tracked her breathing. 

V'ika pulled out his journal and reviewed it before writing an update while Svila watched from the back of the group, scratching absently at their arms, skin crawling from something they couldn't name. Nearby, Xyla wandered to a tree at the rear and leaned against it, arms folded across her chest.

Ayre had a hand pressed to her head, brows pinched together as though fighting something off. She shook it away after a moment. Orlando tightened his gauntlets and flexed his hands, turning his wrists over as he studied the blackened metal. V remained still, poised, staring at the horizon.

Fiora pointed toward the port. "Think there's a ship fit to carry the lot of us?"

"Possibly, though acquiring it will be a challenge given the amount of troops down there," Orlando replied, his monotone further muffled by the bandages wrapped around his face.

Cassian's jaw tightened as he surveyed the patrols. "The Crimson Court is holding this port, and one of those ships is our only way to the Brood Pits." He looked at Fiora. "There has to be. Perhaps that ship. Any ideas?" He put the question to whoever was listening.

Svila's voice came low and uneasy. "Something about this place is... deeply unnerving."

V lowered himself into a crouch, eyes on the water. "I've not seen waters that color. Think it's safe for a swim?" he asked aloud.

Astares, meanwhile, had his carryall open and was working through his itemized list, each medicinal tincture, roll of gauze, dressing, oil, and balm accounted for alongside other procured supplies. He secured his belongings as the rest continued talking.

Xyla overlooked the town, her frown deepening. She moved to a less exposed position and motioned for the group to follow, keeping her voice low. "I don't know, but it's best we try to avoid the guard as much as we can. We don't need to draw undue attention and must keep the element of surprise as best as we can."

One hand on her side, the other gripping Dawnbringer like a lifeline, Fiora limped after Xyla.

At her side in a step, Cassian hummed quietly in thought. "Perhaps we could generate a big distraction somewhere in the port to attract guards there, creating an opening for people to rush to the ship. Perhaps explosives. But getting closer without being spotted still remains difficult."

Ayre turned on her heels toward them. "Are you thinking of explosions or commotion, Cassian?"

"Both," Cassian said.

Astares spoke up, measured and deliberate. "I believe it'd be best if we were to do this, I would believe it more plausible to do so in groups, as in large would provide too much cluster and not notable room to account for situational shortcomings."

With his journal closed and tucked into his bag, V'ika walked toward Xyla. He addressed Cassian with an edge of doubt. "Would a loud distraction be wise?" A pause. "Then again, what other ideas do we have..."

Ayre counted it off on her fingers. "So we need a group to be loud and proud, and a group to sneakily make a bomb or something go off... and then the rest take the ship, and we follow suit... yea? Or am I missing something?"

V's head swiveled, one ear flicking at the suggestion. He settled his elbows on his knees, studying the port below. "A small group, those yet swift of foot should be able to get in and get out before real trouble arises." His eyes traced avenues of ingress, corridors that might create the widest gap for the group to pass through.

A shake of the head from Astares. "No, if we have a third team, it would need to be covered. The teams would ultimately need to work in section to progress effectively and together. Our goal would be to reach the ship as an entire group."

V'ika nodded. "I agree with Astares on that. A third group would be a bit of a mess."

Soot's voice cut through. "Why not go in disguise?"

The admission came reluctantly, as Fiora spoke in a flat tone, "I will not be moving swiftly. Even if a distraction is made, we'll all need to reconvene on the ship and there doesn't seem to be a good point to do so that wouldn't be an obvious point of interception by any guards."

V'ika glanced at her. "You're as light as a feather to me, Fiora. Possibly to Ayre as well. The point is, someone can carry you without much change in their speed. But stealth is a better choice in this case."

"If we can, I'd propose that our more swift on foot characters be our eyes and take out unwarranted eyes not currently accounted for," Astares pressed.

A hum, then a slow nod from Xyla. "A distraction would usually be a good thing, but I think stealth may be the aim right now. And as Astares said, we all need to make it to the ship." Her voice hardened with something between authority and warning. "Remember, I said we all needed to begin working as a team, otherwise I will put you all through the Vanguard version of a bootcamp."

Something had changed. Cassian's hand shifted to his pocket, fingers closing around something that pulsed against his palm. He stood motionless, staring toward the horizon. "What in the Twelve..." he murmured, barely audible. Cold sweat ran down his temple.

A rhythmic tap pressed against the back of his hand. Fiora's brow creased. Her signed message: What is going on?

His face snapped toward her—too fast. Startled. Cassian took a slow breath and returned the taps: The sigil speaks to me.

_The Seventh Sigil of the Crimson Court pulsed with deep, radiant power inside his pocket. Fiora could sense it reacting to something, or someone. It had been dormant until now. Something was waking it._

The touch sent her reeling. Fiora jolted back, shuddered, and the motion sent pain flaring through her side. She doubled over with a gasp.

"Are you well, Cassian?" Orlando asked, his piercing gaze fixed on the man. "It would be dangerous if you were running a fever at a time like this."

Ayre's head whipped around. "So stealth and a distraction... Hmmm... well we all are certainly aware of who amongst us are—" She broke off mid-sentence. "By the Dusk Mother! Fiora?! What the hells is wrong?"

Her answer came thin, breathless. "Something changing... that forsaken sigil..." Fiora shook her head, fighting for composure.

Cassian raised a brow and stepped back from her, concern and caution pulling in equal measure.

Ayre's voice climbed. "Shitshitshit... The hell are you talking about a 'sigil?' This is something I wasn't briefed on again?"

V'ika stared at Fiora. "Are you alright?" The worry was plain.

Steady despite the tension, Svila spoke up. "I could veil a group of three, maybe four, if we want an extra layer of stealth. It wouldn't last long, but enough to get at least a cursory scope of the place." He looked between Astares and Xyla, awaiting instruction.

V studied the black water again. Then the port. Something was forming behind his eyes—a very stupid, small idea. Black catte, black water. What could go wrong?

Down at the docks, two figures caught Xyla's attention. She canted her head, eyes narrowing, and her voice dropped. "The two figures at the dock... one is..." She frowned. "Eerily familiar to me, but I can't place it. He's veiled pretty hard by something that's causing him to be hazy, like..." She exhaled, frustrated. "Coward. Either way, I am not spotting any openings that are completely clear, either." She turned to Fiora. "Are we sure you are okay to be here right now?"

That did it. Fiora straightened. Her hands balled into fists, and her expression went blank, wiped clean of everything. "I am fine. It is better than being left behind to wonder. I am capable enough to continue on." She drew a vial from her pocket, downed it and with it some color returned to her cheeks.

The gathering had drawn Astares's attention, his earlier worries confirmed. "What is happening over he—" He saw the shift in posture, the tension in the air. "All of you step back a little!"

A calming gesture, a shake of the head, and Fiora tossed the empty vial his way. She stood steadier now.

V'ika looked at Astares. "Calm down. We don't need to panic. Especially with where we're at."

"This is not panic, Master V'ika. Understand the situation." Astares held firm.

V'nhea had been studying the town below. "The streets are too narrow for a group to really make any real progress. The only salvation is for the group to keep moving, otherwise a phalanx could lock us in." He motioned toward the water. "That is the only other option I see, and I don't know what lurks in those waters, but swimming to the boat would be the stealthiest approach."


## A Caravan Approaches

Their discussions were cut short. At the entrance to Gloomport, a caravan had begun forming up—twenty soldiers escorting a large cart with a cage on the back. Something moved inside. They were preparing to leave, and if the caravan departed while the group still sat exposed on the ridge, they'd be spotted.

Svila lofted a brow at the approach and inched closer for a better look.

"I believe we must make haste sooner rather than later," Orlando said, turning to face the distant column. "Enemies on the way and I believe too many for us to reasonably fight at our current condition."

Soot watched the road, her eyes tracking the cart. "Uh oh..."

Back at the front of the group, Xyla spotted the caravan and her frown deepened. "We need to move now and get out of sight." She shook her head. "That damn sigil—we'll figure that out once we are out of sight. Move it and don't draw attention to yourselves in the process." Her gaze swept the terrain, searching for cover.

"Let's move then. I'll follow your lead," Cassian said, falling in behind her.

V rose and moved without a word, dropping his blades where they lay. He didn't need them. Not really. He stepped out of the treeline and onto the road, walking with the loose, unhurried gait of a traveler. A stray. Years of a beggar's life had taught him the art of duplicity, to some extent.

The moment Fiora spotted V’s movement, she hissed. "We must move, now."

"I do understand the situation and where we're at. She has just drank something to help her." V'ika turned and saw V already in the open. "Oh for the love of—" He moved quickly.

From her position, Xyla watched V wander toward the road and hissed his name in a hushed tone.

The caravan lurched forward. Screams rose from the cage—raw, desperate cries for help that carried across the distance. Civilians, bound for the same fate as every other soul The Watcher had ground into anima to fuel his works. The column was heading straight for them.

"You all better make a decision real quick," Xyla called. "Do we engage or watch it pass?"

"Engage," Cassian said.

The soldiers escorting the caravan noticed V on the road. They tensed, but didn't reach for their weapons. The column continued forward.

Ayre's voice pitched with urgency. "Can we HANDLE the entire bloody garrison?! I like a fight as much as the next person, so I'm fine fighting—but CAN we? With Fiora wounded?"

"He's under contract, what choice do we have?" Fiora growled. "He'll be the next caught if we leave him."

"Best we aid V," V'ika said.

"Alright, who can handle themselves well enough to act as a distraction?" Ayre was already working through it. "I say we have most of us move to the ship, and the rest stick with V and get that caravan. Get the ship and then get the hell out of here!"

The caravan was almost on top of V now. The lead soldier fixed his gaze on him. "Escaped the tithe, did we? Lucky little thing. Come to play hero, or to run off into the woods and wait for the inevitable?"

Both hands lofted in the air, V called out with a grin. "Hellooooo!" He drew his mask down. "Forgive me. I am not from around here." A gesture at himself, head to toe. "Tithe?" His hands stayed raised, showing no threat. "Where am I?"

"I can help act as a distraction," Orlando replied, pulling the Sanguine Harvester from a tear.

Xyla made the call. "Cassian, take a small group and go back up V." She frowned. "The rest of us start moving towards the ship. Someone able to man a gun if needed."

"Three with me. Not you, Ayre." Cassian was already moving. "Fiora, you're with me."

"What the hells?!" Ayre's protest came sharp and immediate. "Urg, fine!"

"Fiora, Orlando, Soot—with me." Cassian moved toward the caravan.

The lead soldier squinted at V, and something clicked behind his eyes. "Halt!" The caravan ground to a stop. "You're one of them, are you? The outsiders? Oh, we'll be rewarded well for your capture." He glanced back at the soldiers behind him. "Pull one of the cages and put them to the sword. Make room for this one."

Soldiers dismounted and drew weapons, advancing on V. Three remained behind, opening the back of the cage to drag a civilian out for execution.

V moved first. He sprinted forward, swift and sudden, launching himself into the air with his legs sweeping under him as he pivoted, both knees driving forward into the lead soldiers in a flying strike.

"Drop your weapons and stay calm!" Cassian shouted as he closed the distance. "Play nice and we won't be forced to do something we don't want to do."

A strong gust of air lashed out from Fiora's hands, drawing the breath from the nearest soldiers. She was left gasping from the effort.

Chains rattled as Orlando stepped through a Tear and reappeared between three of the caravan guards, the Harvester already tearing through the air. "Do not lie, Cassian. I think I rather enjoy slaughtering slavers." A twist, and the Harvester cleaved through all three.

Soot charged forward, throwing one soldier into another and snapping toward a third, catching his fingers in her mouth.

V's strike caved in the lead soldier's chestplate. The man couldn't breathe. Before he could fall, V tore the armor from his body and flung the piece at the mounted riders, unseating them.

Caught off guard by the party charging from the woods, the escort crumbled. Ten fell in quick succession. The remaining seven at the front rallied and lashed out at Orlando and V, charging with swords drawn. Behind the caravan, the civilians began leaping from the cage, fifteen of them rushing to hide behind the group—a meager tithe, but The Watcher wasn't picky.

Orlando exhaled and dodged the strikes with an almost lethargic pace. "The toys of this realm are rather boring. Mastema, do what you will." He stepped back through a tear as a guard's blade missed his head by a wide margin. Before the soldier could recover, a large taloned hand speared through his stomach from the other side. An inhuman wail echoed across the road as the Avatar's head pushed through the rift and ripped the man apart with its beak.

More tears opened around the four soldiers still advancing on Orlando. A wave of malice radiated from each one as taloned hands reached through, seizing ligaments and sinew. With a sickening crunch and snap, the hands tore them free through the skin and turned the soldiers into macabre puppets, forcing them to fight one another. Orlando watched and clapped along as though it were a circus performance until, one by one, the pain proved too much and the guards died screaming. His laughter threaded through theirs. "Thank you, my friend. That greatly improved my mood," he said, and bowed to the Avatar.

Past Orlando's massacre, Cassian ran toward the remaining soldiers. He leapt and slashed downward through a soldier's chest, then carried the strike through a second. "If there are still soldiers standing, lay down your weapons! Surrender or join the blood pool!"

V ducked and swiveled through sweeping blades, his time on the blood sands having made combat something close to a game for him—cocky and, to some degree, arrogant. Arrogant enough to miss the spearman advancing from his flank. The thrust came fast. His head jerked back just in time to save his face from being impaled, but not fast enough to keep the tip from opening a fresh mark across his cheek. His arms wrapped around the shaft and held it, giving Cassian the opening to cut down the man who'd made him bleed.

A gentle flow of aether wrapped around V, sinking into flesh and mending the wound before it had a chance to drip. Fiora dropped to one knee and the flow waned.

By some miracle—Orlando's butchery had been swift enough—the exchange hadn't drawn the garrison's attention. Every soldier protecting the caravan lay dead. The civilians cowered behind the group, their blank white masks frozen in various degrees of despair and fear. None spoke. They looked around in a panic, mapping routes to escape.


## Gloomport Infiltration

Xyla watched the caravan for a moment, then shook her head. "We have to hope and trust they have it in hand. We need to move to the ship. Let's get going." She scanned the streets below for the best opening through the village.

"Alright... when the hell should we break for the ship?" Ayre's grip shifted on her axe. "Stick behind me. I'll protect us as best I can. If you get caught, just yell. Let's go!"

The streets were empty—for now. Deeper in the town, the sound of boots on cobblestone echoed between buildings. More patrols, moving somewhere they couldn't see. The group had reached the upper district, two districts still separating them from the harbor. And with every step closer to the docks, the ambient anima thickened. Not gradually. It pressed in on them, heavy and wrong, far beyond anything they'd encountered elsewhere. Something was down there.

At the rear, Astares closed his eyes and reached out with his senses, scanning for shifts in the energy around them while covering the group's back.

Xyla shook her head as the atmosphere changed, her frown cutting deeper. "Something isn't right. You all need to be on guard." She pulled her bow from her back and held it ready, keeping to the shadows as they moved. Through the gaps between buildings, she tried to maintain a line of sight on the dock and the shrouded figures stationed there.

The headache Ayre had been fighting all day surged. She gripped her head with one hand, her axe dipping in the other. The anima sat in her lungs like wet cloth. "Nnnn... this anima... it's so heavy... what the hell is doing that?!" The growl in her voice barely stayed contained, forced down to a whisper through gritted teeth.

V'ika's tail puffed out as he scouted their immediate surroundings, trying to piece together a next move.

"We need to change our directive," Astares said, his voice low and tight. "I do not like this presence. I suggest we halt our advances and recon as much as possible. Whatever is ahead of us, I do not like. We need to change our tactic—I do not feel safe about us heading further into that density."

"It's not like whatever it is we can handle it alone, broken up as we are," Ayre shot back. "We need to get to the ship, and maybe once we are all together again we can handle whatever's—Nnnn!—causing this suffocating aether corruption..." She switched to Xaelic under her breath. "By Nhaama..."

Xyla's voice dropped to barely a murmur. "I have a feeling I know who or what is causing it, and it's on the dock. I suspect it'll leave with the ship." She drew the small group closer. "We need to figure this out and fast. Whatever is shrouded on the dock, I am almost certain we've come across before." She stopped and pressed further into the nearest shadow. "We should likely wait and fall back at least a short distance. If we had our scout, we wouldn't be having this issue." A shake of her head.

Her hand went to her ear, attempting to contact the group through the Vanguard linkshell. Static.

Between the nearby houses, a patrol moved block by block, sweeping for anyone they hadn't yet captured. They were heading toward the group. The window to move was closing.

"I think you forget how well your eyes serve you, Lady Xyla," Astares said, measured even now. "We have four pairs amongst us. If anything, let us survey the area here and have someone keep lookout. My best option for this is to station Lady Xyla on post and have either Master V'ika and I support her, whilst the other two comb this area at best. I do not want our company walking into something without us taking the precautionary steps to make it safer."

Over her shoulder, Ayre's gaze found Xyla. "Then what should we do exactly? We have no idea how much longer everyone behind us can hold out. Fiora can barely—" The sound of boots cut her off. Her head snapped toward it, predatory and immediate, grip white-knuckled on the axe.

Once more, Xyla's hand went to her ear.

Through the Vanguard Link, broken by static, her voice came in fragments: "We—" crackle "—a—" crackle "—plan—" crackle "—here. Something—" The rest dissolved into noise. "—Bad."


## Liberation of the Caravan

The linkshell crackled. Fiora pressed a finger to her ear, straining to parse the words through the interference. "Can't understand—" static swallowed the rest. "What is—" More static. "There is—much interference." She waited, frowning. "—here, meet—and regroup?"

No reply came. She kept her distance from the wagon and the civilians, watching the linkshell with growing unease.

With the last of the soldiers dealt with, V climbed onto the wagon and looked back at the cages. "Get these open. I have plans for this wagon." He hauled himself into the driver's seat, pulled the helmetless corpse from the mooring, and let the body drop to the ground.

Orlando approached the cages still slicked in blood, his battle-mania slower to fade than usual. "Be not afraid, for we bring salvation," he announced.

"GET OUT, GET OUT. FLEE. RUN. GET OUT!" V shouted at the civilians from the driver's seat.

Cassian stepped aside to make room, his voice steady against V's shouting. "We're not here to harm you, only them." He pointed at the dead soldiers. "Get out and get to safety."

The civilians stared at V, frozen. Some were eased by Cassian's measured tone and began to move, but one broke in the wrong direction—a disoriented sprint straight toward the town gates.

Cassian lunged and tried to tackle the panicked civilian. He hit dirt, face-first. The man was already pulling away, legs churning toward the gate. Cassian spat earth.

From the wagon, V turned and focused his aether, loosing a levin bolt at the fleeing figure.

"Perhaps they have caught some interesting prey, then," Orlando cackled, already turning to make his way toward the village.

The lone civilian reached the gates.

Soot hurled a rock as hard as she could. It sailed wide.

The civilian made it inside.

Still waiting by the wagon, Fiora pulled the linkpearl from her ear, frowned at it, and pressed it back in. "No response from the other group. Perhaps we should attempt to join them. I'm growing worried at the silence."


## A Menacing Force

V'ika bit his tongue for a moment before speaking in a hushed tone. "They're bringing some civilians towards the exit. We don't have much time. We may get spotted if we don't move."

Fire began pooling around Ayre's hand as she channeled what aether she could, eyes narrowing on the patrol. "Should we engage them? Take them out by surprise?"

Astares nodded to V'ika. "Lady Xyla? Lady Ayre?"

Something came on the breeze. A voice, or the rustling of leaves, or nothing at all. "Ayre..." There might have been a laugh behind it. "I look forward to watching your rage melt into resignation."

On the same wind, another whisper: "Xyla..." That same half-laugh. "Hesitation will be your end."

And a third: "V'ika." Closer now, amused. "I can smell your fear."

V'ika's head jerked, scanning the empty air around him. "I just heard a voice in the wind."

"I did as well," Astares said, fixing his glasses with deliberate calm. "Though prudent we don't listen to such. No one has a harsher tone than that of another I know." A sigh. "We need to prepare ourselves, quickly!"

"Hesitation my arse," Xyla muttered. "More like not running headlong." She glared at the group. "We need to move and we need to get further in. Comms are no use and we are in too deep."

The nearest patrol was one street away.

Ayre swung her fist behind her at the voice, a feral snap at empty air. Nothing there. The growl that rolled through her came low and animal. She turned back toward the guards. "Kill them. We kill them before they see the other group down the road."

The panic bled out of V'ika's posture. Others had heard it too. "Alright. Let's fight then." He settled himself.

"Well," Xyla whispered, "looks like the choice is made. We take this group out."

She drew back the string on her bow, an arrow nocked at head height from her slight vantage point. Waiting.

The moment the words left Xyla's mouth, Ayre moved—axe hoisted onto her shoulders, every muscle coiled, then released. She lunged at the nearest guards with her axe raised to cleave the first one in two.

Behind her, Astares spread his arms. The latch on his grimoire slipped open and the book flew from his carryall, levitating at his side as he cast a barrier over Ayre.

Then the civilian came.

He barreled past the group from behind—loud footsteps, ragged gasps of panic—and three nearby patrols turned at the noise. The first opened fire. An arrow punched through the civilian's left eye and he dropped. But all three patrols had seen more than the fleeing prisoner, they'd noticed the group.

"INTRUDERS! SOUND THE ALARMS!"

Fifteen soldiers drew their swords and charged.

Ayre's voice tore through the street in Xaelic before switching back. "OH FOR FUCK— ALRIGHT! NO MORE BEING QUIET! XYLA, V'IKA, ASTARES, RUN BACK! I'LL COVER OUR ESCAPE! LET'S REGROUP!"

An arrow flew from Xyla's bow, finding a guard as she spoke into her comms.


## Regroup in Gloomport

Alarm bells split the air from inside the port.

The linkshell crackled with Xyla's voice, "F— Wha—" Static swallowed the rest.

Cassian's tone shifted, clipped and commanding. "Run out, fast. Do not go to Gloomport. Things are going to get dangerous there." The remaining civilians listened and began to scatter away from the port, now in full alarm.

Up on the coach seat, V turned the caravan around. He stood, focused his aether, and blasted fireballs at the base of the cage—he'd given everyone ample warning to leave. The wood caught. He took up the reins and cracked them hard, driving the burning caravan back toward the port gates at speed. It hit the side wall with a splintering crash. Didn't make it through. V jumped clear at the last second and landed at the gate.

The others ran after him.

What they found inside stopped them cold. The second group was almost entirely surrounded—fifteen soldiers with swords forming a tight ring while five mages behind them lobbed massive anima blasts overhead and into Xyla's team. No escape route. No options, not without support.

Ayre let out a scream—not pain, rage. She slammed her axe into the ground and pulled on every scrap of aether she could reach. A wall of fire erupted behind her, cutting off the soldiers' advance toward Xyla and the others, funneling them toward her instead. "BRING IT ON YOU PATHETIC WEAKLINGS! I WILL NOT FALL SO EASILY TO YOUR FRAIL MAGICKS!"

Cassian took in the field in a glance, eyes finding the mages in the back. "Orlando, Soot, try to make quick work of those mages if you can. Fiora, support them if they need it. If not, stay near me." He was already running, blade aimed at the nearest caster.

An anima blast caught V'ika square and he stumbled, ears flattening. "Not good, not good!" he hissed, then steadied himself and cast benefic on Xyla.

V came tearing in behind the rest. He spotted the swarm, changed direction, and sprinted—one foot off Orlando's shoulder, then up, launching himself onto the nearest rooftop.

"Hopefully they shall slake my thirst!" Orlando shouted, bracing as V'nhea used his shoulder as a springboard. He stepped through a tear and reappeared beside a mage, the Harvester already falling.

A gust of wind tore from Fiora's hands and caught one of the mages mid-cast, knocking them off the dock's edge. They sank into the black water without surfacing.

"Come at me, useless spellweavers!" Cassian brought his blade down through another mage with a savage downward slash.

A fireball caught Xyla in the shoulder. She grimaced, pulled back, and drew. A solid black shimmering arrow flew straight and buried itself in the nearest guard's eye.

From the rooftop, V began dropping bricks into the soldiers below. No pattern, no aim worth mentioning. Just bricks, raining down at random.

The mages fell, then five of the swordsmen with them. The sudden assault from both sides split the formation, half of them peeling away from Ayre.

Bricks kept raining from the rooftop as V hurled them without pattern or mercy. Below, Xyla drew and loosed again—another shimmering black arrow, another guard's eye. Ayre spun on her feet, axe sweeping wide, but five blades came at her before the rotation finished. She turned each one aside and let the arc carry through, cleaving those still standing close enough to reach.

Dawnbringer flared. Fiora pulled another vial from her pack, downed it, and broke open the ground beneath the soldiers nearest to Astares. V'ika was already turning on one of them—cards flew from his deck in a vicious spread, edges shredding through the man before snapping back to the deck like they'd never left. A mote of light drifted from Astares toward Xyla in return, settling over her wounded shoulder with a gentle glow.

"Make your first strike count, because I'm not giving you a second," Cassian told the soldiers as his blade nicked armor and slashed through an arm beneath it. Behind them, Orlando slipped through another Tear and appeared at a soldier's back. The Harvester's chains rattled in the wind as the blade sank home.


## All is Quiet on the Gloomport Front

The last soldier fell, and with him came silence. The bells went quiet. No further patrols arrived. Odd—there had been far more soldiers in Gloomport than what they'd fought, and these ones had been weak. Front-line fodder.

The anima pressed heavier than ever. For those who'd only just entered the town after the caravan, the difference was staggering—thick enough to feel its weight on their shoulders, in their chests. Something was deeply, unmistakably wrong.

"Let's take advantage of the silence and move up towards the ship," Cassian said, steadying his footing against the oppressive air. "Be ready for everything. Sounds good?"

"Anyone who is injured or who needs medical attention, please see myself and Master V'ika," Astares said. "That goes for you, Lady Fiora, and Master Cassian. Let us assess the rest as a priority after. Master V'ika?"

V'ika stored his globe and fell in beside Astares with a nod.

"Something's wrong," Ayre said, breathing hard. "Terribly wrong. Are we certain this is wise? There's something out there that's making the very air difficult to breathe... at least for me it is."

When the last echo of combat faded, Fiora sank to her knees. She dug through a pocket for another vial, downed it with a grimace. It hung loosely in her fingers as she sat with her head between her knees.

Cassian moved to her side, eyes still scanning the rooftops and alleys around them.

Astares leaned down and snatched the vial from her hand the moment she wasn't looking, shaking his head. "Lady Fiora... what is this?" A sigh.

"Ether potions, supplementary," she muttered into her knees. "Mine is drained and this place is empty of it."

"Her staff generates it," Cassian said, looking at Astares, "but the potions help boost her when this place leaves her feeling drained."

"There's a reason I'm not worried about it, Astares," V'ika said. "At least, not right now, due to her state."

"Are you able to stand, Lady Fiora?" Astares asked. Concern bled through his tone, but he pressed on. "We can't leave you here, and we need to press on once we have discussed our next move." He nodded to V'ika. "Yes, but you need to understand, I need to account for everything."

Fiora kept her head cradled between her knees, eyes squeezed shut. "Back of my pack, another vial... I should be able to move after. We already discussed this—if they have their shipments, we are at a disadvantage at the pass."

Cassian rummaged through her pack, found the vial, and handed it over. He helped her to her feet and walked beside her, steadying her as the potion took hold.

"Master V'ika, her vial... please." Astares leaned down to monitor Fiora as she stood.

Up on the rooftop, V pulled out one of his cigarettes and sparked it. He took several long drags, watching the angry cherry glow at the tip, blue smoke curling upward in soft billows. His eyes moved across the roofline, looking for anything flammable.

For the first time since arriving, Orlando went still. He let the energy of this place settle over him and tensed immediately. His eyes moved first, then his head—listening, breathing deep. The weight was manageable, but something else had his attention. "I feel we are being observed," he said quietly, grip tightening on the Harvester.

Xyla's frustration boiled over. "I'm going to kill Z'iro for not being here, along with anyone that was with us when we met whoever that is on the dock." She was mumbling, angry, but loud enough for the group. "There is someone on the dock that we have encountered before. I have a feeling it's 'The Collector.'" Air quotes around the name. "We fought him once and he vanished. We hoped he was dead, but..." A pause. "We may have been wrong." She looked back toward where the dock lay hidden beyond the buildings. "Alright, we can't sit here. What do people want to do—retreat or push further?"

On the roof, V hummed softly to himself. The wind shifted but the oppressive air remained. He plucked the cigarette from his lips and watched the smoke drift upward, his voice barely a murmur—more to himself than anyone below.


## Weight of Anima

While the others crowded around Fiora, Ayre slipped away.

She closed her eyes, drew a slow breath, let it out. That voice—sweet and sickening, threading through the anima like silk through tar. There might have been truth in it. And maybe, if she moved quickly, she could find its source. She wouldn't be gone long. Just enough to scout ahead.

The whisper came again, faint, carried on a dying breeze. "You stand alone even now," it mused. Laughter, distant and soft. "You owe them no loyalty. Do they flock to your aid? No. Do they check on you now? No, no they do not. Come to the docks while their attention is turned. Now is your opportunity to seize everything you've always wanted."

The weight of the anima lifted, just slightly, an invitation to continue onward. The streets ahead were empty, dead. Husks of fallen civilians littered the road.

Ayre moved through them with sharp eyes, scanning the bodies as she passed. The lighter air made it easier to focus, easier to breathe, easier to keep going.

Then the presence returned. Heavy, filling the air around her like water filling a room. It didn't unnerve her, but her guard held. Flames ignited along the blade of her axe. She was poised to strike.

Two figures stood at the end of the dock. Their forms were a haze, obscured by something she couldn't name. And then, without warning, the weight of the anima came crashing down like a tidal wave. Everything the group had felt was nothing compared to this. It was choking. It was the world falling on top of her. And beneath the crushing pressure, a burning certainty that though both figures faced away, one of them was keenly, intimately aware of her.

The pressure drove Ayre to her stomach, flat, unable to move. The air forced from her lungs in a single, silent rush. The voice returned. "Rage first... and give me... resignation." That laugh, sweet and melodic.

She couldn't even growl. Rage—it was all she had left, and she would not let it curdle into surrender. On the ground, crippled, she wanted to claw at the stone, do something, anything, but the pain built so fast that even her sister's voice was screaming inside her skull.

Orlando had also walked off, unnoticed. The town was dead around him. Empty streets, empty windows. The weight of the anima multiplied with every step, vicious and heavy. Sweat broke across his skin. Far down the road ahead, Ayre lay crumpled in a heap.

A voice drifted on the dying breeze, faint and welcoming. "Join your friend, Orlando. Be the hero."

He tilted his head to one side, unnerved but steady. He pulled a cigar from his coat and unwound the bandages from his face, lighting up. A cloud of smoke curled into the dead air. "Wrong to assume I am a hero."

The anima kept building. It crashed into him like a wave, forcing him to his knees. The voice carried on the breeze once more. "Watch your friend suffer. A villain's reward."


## We'll Cross That Bridge

"Are we all accounted for?" Fiora asked after finishing the vial, steadier on her feet again.

"Astares, I just want to say—perhaps ask me if I know something in the future before going to such actions. I don't mean this to be rude, but someone may have a bad reaction if you do that again." V'ika looked around the group, then stopped. His tail shot up, puffed out. "Where's Ayre?"

"Perhaps she's further ahead," Cassian said once Fiora was upright, already moving. "Let's keep going." He signed up to V, motioning for him to rejoin the group.

Behind the group, Astares shook his head and muttered to himself. "And yet he still does not get it."

On the rooftop, V caught Xyla's attention. She frowned, watching him curiously.

V stepped off the side of the building, caught the edge, and dropped two stories to the ground with a heavy thud.

"We should continue pushing ahead while it's quiet," Cassian said to Xyla. "I don't like the eerie feeling, but we don't have another choice."

"Quiet doesn't always mean it's good," V'ika said.

Astares fell to the rear to cover their back as the group pressed forward.

"True enough," Xyla said with a shrug, "but I at least warned you about what's ahead."

They reached a bridge. V hummed, studying it. Then he stepped to the side, flipped himself over the railing, and began traversing hand-over-hand along the underside. Stealth, his way.

The weight of the anima kept multiplying. It had become more than physical, greatly affecting those attuned to aether. It pressed against the mind, thick and suffocating, worsening with every step deeper into the town.


## One Trap is as Good as Another

Behind the main group, Astares and V formed the rearguard. V pulled himself back up from under the bridge and looked toward the rooftops. "Something is off."

"You feel it too?" Astares asked. "What do you sense, V?"

"A trap," V states without elaborating.

Astares responds, "I couldn't put my finger on it at first, but it becomes clearer the further we trek. I can sense the pressure and it is getting denser."

"It's what I would do." V glanced up.

Astares sighed. "Lure..." His gaze followed the group already advancing ahead, moving out of sight. His head dropped. "And we are walking right into it."

"Go on," V said. He paused, then lifted his hand and smashed a window pane. Another cigarette, eighteen left. He sparked it, drew until the cherry burned hot and angry, and shoved the lit end through the broken window. A fire trail.

He nodded. "Shall we, then?" He began to move, slow enough for Astares to keep pace.

"I won't be without you with me," Astares said firmly. "We have already misplaced two people. We cannot add to it. We go together, or we stay together."

V shrugged and focused his aether one last time, gathering a ball of flame in his palm, and slammed it down into the bridge behind them. The wood splintered and caught. It cut off anyone following—but it also cut off their retreat.

The heat washed over them both, bright enough to gleam off Astares's spectacles. He adjusted his glasses. "I understand."

V watched the flames for a moment, making sure they took. They did, slowly, struggling against the dead air. He turned and followed after the group.


## Under Pressure

The pressure closed around Fiora like a box shrinking inward, pressing against her aether, trying to smother it entirely. Dawnbringer pulsed in her grip, the relic held the worst of it at bay, but only just. She stopped. One hand grasped her chest, the other white-knuckled around the staff. She leaned into Cassian, and when he took another step forward she couldn't follow. Her feet went backward instead. Cassian buried beneath the earth, the image flashed behind her eyes, vivid and unbidden. Another stumbling step back.

Cassian caught her weight and held. "Hang in there. What's going on?" He kept moving, slow, pulling her with him.

Beside them, V'ika hissed through his teeth and grabbed at his own body as though trying to hold himself together. He stumbled back, ears flat, tail bristling.

"Pressure..." Fiora managed, dragging her feet forward as Cassian pulled. "So much, smothering."

"Whatever is ahead doesn't want us to get to it. It's pushing you back." Cassian shifted, crouching. "Let's keep moving." He lifted Fiora onto his back and carried her.

V'ika pushed forward but lagged behind with every step.


---

In Ayre's mind, the voice was no longer a whisper. It was a boom, rattling through her skull. "You are lost. So far from home, little Ayre."

A pathetic growl was all her body could manage. But inside her head, two versions of herself stood facing each other—one with her blue eye blazing, the other lit red. The red one spoke, rage given form. "You... bastard... not even... fighting fair! You think I don't know I'm far from the Azim Steppe?! You're going to die slowly and painfully when I get up!"


---

Xyla kept watch as they crossed the bridge, eyes narrowed. She glanced over her shoulder, noticed the gaps in the group, and stopped. Waiting.

They reached the crest where Orlando knelt, sweat-slicked, unable to rise. The anima bore down on each of them now as though the world itself sat on their shoulders. For those attuned to aether, breathing had become an act of will. And beneath the weight, something else—every one of them could feel it now, individually, directly. Something was watching them.

Further down the road, Ayre lay in a heap, crushed under the weight of something unseen.

As V and Astares caught up to the main procession, Xyla's eyes narrowed at the pair. "What took you two so long? We shouldn't be splitting up."

"We weren't too far along... sorry to worry you," Astares said with a nod.

V kept walking, his gaze fixed on a building glow in the distance. "Chaos is birthed from strategic points of order. I would see it has the best chance at life."

"Trust you." Xyla smirked, shook her head, and pressed on, listening, watching for whatever might come next.

At the bridge railing, V'ika leaned hard against the stone, barely moving.

"Master V'ika, are you okay?" Astares asked, his concern plain.

V'ika shook his head. "Too much pressure... how can you stand it?"

"I could sense it long before we trekked here. I have experiences with denser energies. It isn't easy, but you will get the better of it in no time. Here, with me." Astares walked at his side, helping him down the bridge to rejoin the rest.

V'ika nodded and kept close.

Every step threatened to drive Cassian to his knees, but Fiora's weight on his back kept him upright, failing meant both of them. One step. Then another. "Coooomeeee onnnnn... we must... reach the ship..."

Struggling under the weight, Fiora wriggled free from Cassian’s back and drove Dawnbringer into the ground with purpose, planting the staff like an anchor. "Please, let this work." She drew aether to her—a long, straining moment—then expelled it outward in a wide pulse.

The crushing weight loosened. Not much. Enough for Orlando to stand, enough for Ayre to push herself upright. The presence remained heavy around them all, but Fiora had carved a margin to breathe.

Cassian slipped an arm around her waist and took her weight again as they continued forward.


## We Meet Again

The laugh tore through Ayre's mind. "The names of your world mean nothing. Soon, it will be his." The voice shifted, almost genuinely sympathetic. "I am sorry, Ayre, that it had to be this way. You could have all died in silence, oblivious. But you chose this road."

The anima's grip loosened. Not by much. Enough.

Ayre brought her hands to her chest, teeth gritted, and pushed herself off the ground. Blood ran from her nose and mouth, her body straining against something invisible and immense. She held in a plank, shaking, eyes blazing as she stared down the dock at the two figures still facing away. The words came in Xaelic, forced through clenched teeth. "You're... not... killing... my... new... family!"

Orlando rose slowly to his feet, scowling as he made his way to her. "Tis heavy indeed, friend," he said, and reached down to help her up.

Nearby, V eyed the next window and tried the latch instead of breaking it.

As the rest of the group arrived, Ayre found herself able to stand. Two figures waited at the end of the dock—one in black, the other in red and gold. One was familiar. Hauntingly so, to a few.

The figure in black turned. The haze fell away. The Collector looked over the group with passive interest. "And so they arrive. Some new faces, some old. Others are missing. The outcome is still certain." A pause. "Hello, old friends." His tone was flat, but arrogance threaded through it.

Then the second figure began to turn.

"So it would seem." His voice was the one they'd heard in their minds on the road here. The moment his gaze fell upon them, the anima became absolute. Crushing. Every member of the group buckled under it, struggling to stay upright.

"Welcome to my region. I suspect I need no introduction." He studied them. "You've become something of an annoyance, recently. My pet, my captains, my terminal. Temporary relief for you, to be certain, but the outcome remains unchanged. You've done nothing to set back the clock." A sigh. He shook his head. "His plans—our plans—remain unchanged." The words carried something close to sadness, though not for himself. For them.

"You are ill prepared to face me." He turned his back on the group, and the weight lifted. "I have a feeling, though, eventually there will be a reckoning. And for all the foolishness of the notion, I look forward to watching you stumble after me. Your chances of success are near impossible. But perhaps we'll meet again."

He waited for no response. A red portal opened before him. He stepped through, and it closed.

The Collector remained. He tilted his head to one side. "You won't make it to him. I'll be certain to see to that. But I want you to watch—for the best is yet to come." And like his master, he turned and departed the same way.

The anima vanished entirely. The ruined town sat silent around them, the boat waiting at the dock to carry them toward the Brood Pits.


## What Remains

V'ika was the first to recover. His ears relaxed, his tail settled. "Thank you, Astares." His voice was warm beneath the exhaustion. He turned his attention to the group, listening.

The moment the pressure lifted, Ayre's scream ripped through the dock—feral, blood-curdling, the sound of rage held prisoner and finally released. She snatched a stone from the ground and hurled it into the sea. It flew far and splashed deep. She twitched, her red eye blazing. The group watched, uncertain if she was still herself.

Orlando stared at where The Collector had stood, stifling his own fury behind another drag of his cigar. "The future looks so interesting," he said, smoke curling around his face.

V felt himself lighten as the weight left. He mulled over what the two had said, punched out a final window pane, and dropped a lit cigarette into the building. "I liked them," he commented. "Snappy dressers, like yours truly." He tugged at the collar of his coat.

"Fashion sense aside," Astares said, "we still need to account for how dangerously close we came to losing some of our people. And we have yet to acclaim anything useful outside of this scenario. We have searched for no information, no use of knowledge, let alone an inkling on what we are walking into." A sigh.

V glanced back at the fires he'd set. "Well... better start looking."

Fiora stumbled as the weight lifted and looked to Xyla. "Who was that?"

"I fucking knew it." Xyla glared at where the two figures had stood, but held her tongue until they were gone. "One of them was 'The Collector.' The other, I assume, was 'The Watcher.' The Collector we've encountered several times before. We even fought him when we returned our old captain to the depths. But as I said earlier, he vanished. Some of us assumed he was dead. The smarter of us assumed he'd resurface, and it appears we were correct."

She looked at each of them in turn. "I am not sure we are all prepared to face either of them as we stand. Even if we went across, we'd need support from the rest of the lands, or the allies at the very least that we've made." A frown. "I fear we are missing something along the way to make this a little easier on us, and as much as I love a challenge, I have a very bad feeling. The next time I encounter that bastard, I don't want him vanishing again."

None of them saw what happened to Cassian until it was too late.

The instant The Watcher had made eye contact, the sigil in Cassian's pocket ignited—a searing heat that radiated upward through his entire nervous system. He let out a choked, guttural scream, hands flying to the sides of his head as though trying to hold his skull together.

His knees hit the dirt as he desperately struggled for air. Images he'd never seen, voices he shouldn't know—they drowned everything until his strength snapped. One last exhausted exhale, and he collapsed into the dust. Silent. Catatonic.

"Shit!" V'ika sprinted to him and dropped beside him, checking him over.

Xyla turned, looking down at Cassian with open concern. "That is exactly what I mean. It doesn't bode well, and I am not sure we should proceed." Her hand went to her ear—trying the linkpearl again.

"I don't see anything wrong physically," V'ika said, looking up. "Astares?"

Astares knelt beside Cassian, one hand hovering above him, eyes closed. He ran an aetheric scan across Cassian's core. Nothing amiss, at least nothing his senses could find. "Hmmm..."

Against the nearest wall, Fiora slid to the ground, too spent to blink. The only sign of her worry was her clenched fist and the hard set of her jaw.

"Let's move him," Astares said. "Lady Fiora, please, I need you to hold onto me. Master V'ika?" A nod.

V'ika nodded back, then picked Cassian up and slung him over one shoulder like a sack of grain, careful enough not to bruise him, firm enough not to drop him. "Aye."

Fiora blinked blearily at Astares. The cumulative toll of the ether potions and everything she'd spent left her hollowed out. Moments later, Astares had her as well, carrying her off.

V slipped into one of the houses before the fires took hold, raiding what food and supplies he could find as Orlando sat down on the ground, closed his eyes, and exhaled. "Let me know when we depart."

When Xyla finally reached the linkpearl, her voice was clipped and hard. "Captain, we have a situation, and it's likely best if you join us as soon as possible. Skyas and any of the others that are still with us need to come also. We need everyone we can gather." A long, angry sigh. "The Collector lives."

Z'iro's reply was immediate. "On route."

Six hours passed. The group rested in the ruins of Gloomport while they waited.
