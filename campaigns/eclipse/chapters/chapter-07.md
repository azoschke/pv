## Polite Prodding

The crimson moon cast its baleful glow through the windows of the abandoned house, painting Sir Alfred George Tyranius Maximus III's ornate top hat and ludicrously oversized mustache in shades of blood and rust. The mustachioed chest sat before the Phoenix Vanguard, its many tentacles arranged in what might have been an approximation of dignity, had it not been such an utterly absurd sight.

The taciturn Viera fixed Cassian with an expectant stare, silently urging him to speak. Behind him, Fiora's expression had soured, arms crossed as she glared daggers at her husband's back, a silent warning as clear as if she'd shouted it.

Cassian glanced around the room before addressing their peculiar host. "Then, Sir Alfred, did you mention something about a 'damn eclipse ritual'?" He made air quotes with his hands.

Around them, the rest of the company maintained varying degrees of attention. Ren Kusushi stood silently, head tilted in quiet observation, while Visaki regarded their menacing surroundings with visible apprehension. "I'd think this was the climax, if I didn't know better," Visaki murmured.

Skyas watched with amusement, having most certainly been present the entire time and missed nothing of importance. Noticing Fiora's disapproving stare at Cassian, he offered her husband a devious smirk, as if encouraging whatever she was silently warning against. Meanwhile, Rove maintained a safe distance from the animated chest that had initially mistaken him for a corpse, watching with undisguised curiosity but no intention of becoming a snack for a talking box.

"I did, yes," Sir Alfred replied after a thoughtful "Hmmmmm." His lid moved in approximation of speech as his cultured voice continued. "Though that is about all I know of it, that the citizens of this realm were being used to fuel this ritual. It is not the doing of The Watcher, though he is however a member of the Crimson King's high council. I cannot speak to what they intend, that is beyond me, what I do know however is a great deal of what is going on in this region. Of that, I can be of service."

V'ika remained visibly uncomfortable, his tail puffed out and swaying slowly as he took out his journal, meticulously recording every word exchanged. Across the room, Fiora waved a hand at the chest, her expression remaining unchanged despite the gesture that invited further explanation, while Xyla stood back, arms folded across her chest, her gaze fixed intently on Sir Alfred.

"Strange chest, sitting in middle of empty town," the taciturn Viera scoffed. "Have doubts about knowledge. Wonder if faulty."

Cassian crossed his arms in a relaxed pose, ignoring the viera's skepticism. "Alright then, we're all ears. Any information would help us. Any place we should go to or avoid? What dangers lie ahead? What's the Watcher doing and how can we find him? Or whatever you might offer to us, noble Sir Alfred."

Fiora's eyes narrow at Cassian's fawning, as she makes a low scoffing noise at the back of her throat.

Sir Alfred huffed at the taciturn Viera's dismissal before addressing Cassian's questions. "Those are very broad questions. This entire region is a danger, there is little The Watcher cannot see. If you want any hope to defeat him, there is no particular place you -can- avoid." The chest shifted slightly, its tentacles gesturing toward the window. "Look to the horizon, you see those six crimson crystals? They power the shield over the observatory, each situated within a military camp, the largest of which is home to his Lieutenant, The Collector."

"The more we talk, the more he sees what is coming, if this is the case. Unless there are limits to his vision," Fiora grumbled, her scowl deepening.

"Maybe he's just a bad lip reader," C'verna suggested with characteristic dry wit. "Unless he's also the Listener as well." Nearby, Ren remained silent, arms crossed and wearing a slight grin as he observed the proceedings.

"How do we hide ourselves from the Watcher's gaze?" the taciturn Viera asked, cutting to the practical heart of the matter.

Cassian's jaw tightened slightly as he processed the information. "Each camp's likely to have patrols, scouts, and choke points. Do you know any weakness or have any advice for us as to how to lower the shield? Intel is thin." He glanced at the others before returning his attention to Sir Alfred. "And The Collector, what do we know about him?"

V'ika appeared more confused than before, his writing slowing as he struggled to keep pace with the conversation.

Sir Alfred considered for a long moment before speaking. "You cannot hide, but you can, for lack of better terms, poke him in the eye." His tentacles shifted in what might have been a gesture of explanation. "To answer both of your questions, if I were you, I would start with The Conductor, the one who runs the train which nearly hit you upon your arrival. You'll find him at the end of the line to the north, that is where he offloads the nobility class inspecting the region and loads harvested anima—and people, on to be sent to the palace."

Skyas' ears perked up at the mention of the Collector, his posture shifting as he listened more intently, clearly eager for another confrontation.

"AssThe womanng you can cut off their ability to import and export," Sir Alfred continued, "this should weaken his gaze temporarily on the region, as he also brings anima to the observatory through the same train. That said, those military camps are heavily guarded. Be prepared for a fight."

"So we hit the line first. Disrupt their transport, their flow of resources, and rattle the cage." Cassian glanced toward the others before returning his attention to their unusual informant. "Know how many numbers we could be dealing with? Defenses, schedules, anything else?"

The taciturn Viera's attention suddenly shifted to Rove. "Chance to prove usefulness. Have explosives in bags?" 

V'ika looked over at the Viera with his ears flattened, clearly uncomfortable with the mention of explosives, while Visaki leaned forward with interest. "If we need to engage in sabotage, consider me interested," he interjected, a sly grin spreading across his face.

"What!? Why would I carry something so volatile for just a random happenstance!" Rove looked up at the Viera with shock. "I'll have you know I keep my bombcores stashed away elsewhere for the safety of myself and those around me!"

"Then what else do you have that would work to create a distraction?" the taciturn Viera pressed, their gaze unrelenting.

Sir Alfred chuckled. "Well, no, I don't have the answer to that. I've been here, I wasn't keen on being harvested."

"...so you're saying you actually own explosives..?" V'ika asked Rove, his voice barely above a whisper.

"I mean... I don't NOT own some bombcores for the occasional need... just.. not here... right now...." Rove replied, glancing around nervously, as if checking for authorities who might punish him for such admissions. He seemed momentarily to have forgotten they were no longer in Eorzea.

"You uh... You've been in trouble for that one before, haven't you?" Skyas chuckled at the lalafell's obvious discomfort.

The taciturn Viera stared flatly down at Rove, clearly unamused by his evasive answers, while V'ika, visibly concerned about Rove's casual mention of explosives, buried himself in his journal again.

"Fair enough," Cassian said with a dry huff of laughter. His gaze drifted toward a window as he considered their options. "Then we'll have to go in blind. Scout the line ourselves. We'll track the train north, find a lookout point, find out what we're up against before we strike."

Fiora opened her mouth to speak, then closed it again, evidently thinking better of whatever comment had come to mind.

"Excellent," Sir Alfred replied, shifting his peculiar form slightly. "And I shall be here awaiting your safe return and with the expectation that once The Watcher is dealt with, I'll be given safe passage from this realm!"

"Aye, a swift passage to another realm, blazing a trail..." Fiora muttered under her breath, her skepticism evident.

"I mean, it's not like someone said, 'hey Rove, you should bring your COLLECTION OF BOMB CORES WITH YOU TO THE SPOOKY PLACE,' what do you want from me!?" Rove's voice rose as he defended himself against the taciturn Viera's intimidating stare.

"Thank you for the information, Sir Alfred," Cassian said, redirecting the conversation. He turned to the group. "Anyone else have anything to add before we decide to move out? Z'iro? Xyla?"

"We will take that as a no, then," the taciturn Viera grumbled, casting a skeptical glance toward the chest and its conditions for safe passage.

Xyla grinned to herself. "Time not being on our side given the circumstances, I'd have suggested we blow part of the track away. I doubt they can fix that in just a few hours given how heavy those beams can be, even if they do have a little... magical spark on their side." She patted the pouches at her waist. "If nothing else, I am sure one of my concoctions can cause a nice blaze—or if we find some black powder, that can cause a nice bang." Her tone remained casual as her gaze shifted between the group, Sir Alfred, and the window.

The taciturn Viera pointed to Xyla while glancing meaningfully at Rove. "That is what we wanted from you." They offered Xyla a slight nod of approval, suggesting they'd had similar thoughts about sabotaging the tracks. Rove crossed his arms in a pout, clearly feeling it unfair he should be expected to carry explosives for others' convenience.

"Did you spot any supplies on your earlier reconnaissance?" Fiora asked, turning to face the taciturn Viera.

"Village seems long-empty. Taken by Collector, maybe," Taci replied with a solemn shake of their head.

V'ika closed his journal, his writing finished but his discomfort with the explosive plans evident in his silence. Meanwhile, Skyas shifted his weight, restless energy radiating from his posture. "Sounds like our only option here is cutting off the supplies and taking out the Watcher then. But how exactly 'absolute' is his gaze? Won't he see us getting ready to do this?"

"So it seems," Fiora replied curtly. She glanced down at her palm, then clenched it shut before rummaging through her bag for a neatly labeled blue vial. She uncorked it hastily and downed the contents in a single gulp, wiping her mouth before shoving the empty container back into her pack with a grimace.

"Well, we have a place outside of this realm, a few in fact," Xyla said, raising a brow as she turned back to Sir Alfred. "I am sure we could come to some arrangement, and the Captain there wouldn't object—he does like the weird and wonderful things we find."

Z'iro gave a casual shrug. "I'm good with it. Seems like we should get underway, however. I'll meet you all at the bridge when you're ready." He turned and began walking away, taking time to examine their surroundings more carefully than when they'd entered.

Xyla turned to Skyas. "I assume the trains run on some sort of back and forth scheduling. We could work around that and take out the tracks before it's due to arrive. It's twofold, we can either hit when we know it'd be emptier, or hit when it's more full and perhaps get some of those crystals to examine and potentially more information." She shrugged. "After a fight, of course, because I doubt the train's occupants will be too forthcoming."

"I mean I'm just saying," Skyas replied, "it seems to me like if we can figure out just how much he can see, we might be able to split our efforts up. A few of us who have his attention blow up the train while the others ambush him. He wouldn't be expecting us to attack as soon as we've taken out their supplies."

"Splitting up is foolish," Fiora called back sharply as she walked off.

"You split up, you die," Cassian added with finality.

"..death don't sound fun.." V'ika muttered, shrinking into himself.

Rove continued to pout until he realized the others were leaving. The prospect of being left alone with the unsettling chest quickly changed his mind, and he hurried after the group.

"Alright, walking up the line, then?" Cassian asked, looking to Z'iro and Xyla for confirmation.

"Someone grab the small one, lest he be left behind," Fiora instructed without looking back.

"Well excuuuuse me for thinking we could do this a bit quicker..." Skyas pouted, trailing after the others. "And also I wanted the fun job of fighting The Watcher..."

Z'iro leaned off the bridge railing and nodded to the group. "Sounds like the plan, let's get to it." He walked over and once more picked Rove up by the back of his shirt, carrying him like a small suitcase. Rove had apparently given up fighting this treatment, seeming to accept that being carried was preferable to taking so many more steps with his shorter legs.


## Of False Kings and Knights

The group returned to the tracks that cut through the dark woods. Nothing had changed since they'd left—the air remained still, the crimson moon hanging overhead like a watchful eye as they gathered at the split in the path.

"We should have asked how often the trains run," Fiora observed with a frown.

"I asked about schedules. Sir Alfred didn't answer that," Cassian reminded her, staring at the northern line while rubbing his chin thoughtfully.

"We find out for ourselves, then. Shall we continue?" the taciturn Viera prompted.

Z'iro looked down at Rove, pursing his lips slightly. With a sigh, he heaved the lalafell up and perched him on his shoulder like a parrot, nestled among the furs. It was a comfortable seat, at least until Steven returned to likely attack Rove for occupying his usual spot.

"Let's stay off the tracks themselves," Fiora grumbled. "I'd rather not be caught by surprise, nor can I fix pancake."

"We'll follow the tracks on foot, but not on them, we're too exposed," Cassian agreed. "Parallel the line through the treeline, stay out of sight, sounds good? Yes, what Fiora said."

Fiora fixed her gaze on Xyla and Z'iro, waiting for their move or orders, whichever came first. After a moment, Z'iro shrugged and stepped off the tracks into the treeline, accepting the group's decision. He walked far enough to the side that he'd be difficult to spot from the tracks themselves.

As they walked, a faint clinking sound reached their ears—metal against metal, like several pairs of footsteps approaching along the tracks. Whoever it was made no attempt at stealth, the rhythmic sounds echoing through the still forest.

Another viridian glow surrounded Hearthorn as the taciturn Viera focused, summoning a familiar whispering wind that dampened noise around the group, making it harder for the approaching figures to detect them.

"Armor," Cassian muttered under his breath, glancing at the others. "Three of them, moving this way." He unslung his weapon, readying it. "If they see us, we should strike. Fast, clean. We can't let them know we're coming."

"We are being watched, he knows everything anyway," Fiora rolled her eyes but readied her staff regardless.

"'Bout time..." Skyas placed a hand on his sword hilt, a grin spreading across his face. "I thought we were sorely lacking some action."

"Shit," Xyla whispered, her voice barely audible. "Cloaked and hooded well, I can't tell if weapons or not, but they also appear to be keeping to the tracks. Could be looking for us maybe?"

V'ika remained silent, his ears swiveling as he listened to the group's observations, while Cassian muttered to Fiora, "I'd rather assume our position is known but still act as if it isn't, just in case. I'm not sure how the Watcher might communicate with his soldiers, but if there's a chance they don't know..."

The figures continued to approach, their metal boots clanking rhythmically against the tracks. Each was cloaked from head to toe, the sound giving away their armored state. They moved in silence otherwise, occasionally glancing around. As they drew closer, the group could see they were indeed armed, though they hadn't yet spotted their watchers. Meanwhile, Ren stood silently with his eyes closed, his breathing slowing in silent meditation, perhaps in anticipation of what might come.

Cassian crouched lower in the shadows, his fingers tightening around his axe. "They're getting too close," he whispered just loudly enough for the others to hear. "We act now or risk being spotted. We take them quietly. Fast and together." He nodded, preparing to spring into action.

The group stopped.

The trio glanced around, one of them slowly approaching where the Vanguard was hiding, though he didn't seem certain of what he'd heard. After a moment, he took several more steps forward, squinting toward Skyas's hiding place. C'verna reached for her sword as the group turned away from her, minimizing her movements to avoid making noise.

The hooded figure slowly began reaching for his sword.

The taciturn Viera adjusted their grip on Hearthorn and leapt into the air, descending upon one of the remaining two guards still on the tracks, while Cassian jumped out behind Skyas, swinging his axe at the armored figure reaching for his sword.

Fiora retreated several paces, watching Cassian and Skyas leap forward. Drawing on the aether pooling in the Dawnbringer, she shielded Cassian, though her disapproval radiated from her entire being.

The knight that Skyas attacked barely had time to draw his sword before being struck. He fell back, only to be hit again by Cassian and the taciturn Viera. Though he quickly regained his footing, the other knights turned at the commotion. "They're upon us, to arms, brothers!" the injured one shouted. The other two drew their swords and unhooked their shields, charging forward to form a shield wall between their wounded comrade and the attackers.

C'verna, rather than joining the fray, pulled out a stick of dried meat, taking the opportunity to enjoy a quick snack while watching the battle unfold.

The first knight struggled to stand while his companions pressed forward. One slammed his shield into Skyas, knocking him down, while the second engaged Cassian, crouching behind his shield as he rushed him. The knight standing over Skyas pointed his blade downward. "As is deserving of a minion of the false king," he declared, while his comrade locked shields with Cassian, trying to push past the axe with little success. The injured knight finally regained his feet, preparing to rejoin the fight.

"M-must we fight!?" V'ika called out, his voice trembling. "Can we just talk, possibly?"

"False king?" Xyla's brow furrowed as she backed away, drawing her bow. "Wait... who's the true king? Isn't there only the one that's been causing us shit?" She held her aim, watching the knights warily.

Fiora, seeing Skyas fall, reinforced a solid aetheric shield over his prone figure, too focused on maintaining her protection to register the knight's odd statement.

"To the flanks, split them!" Cassian called as he swung again, his brow arched in confusion. "False wha-?"

The taciturn Viera, initially driven by survival instinct, suddenly hesitated as something about the situation registered as strange. Rather than pressing their advantage, they shifted to a defensive stance, watching cautiously. Meanwhile, Rove, in a fit of fear, threw himself backward off Z'iro's shoulder, landing flat on his back with a pained "Oowww... why's he gotta be so tall..." as he struggled to assess the chaotic situation.

Ren kept his hand on his weapon's hilt, positioning himself to charge if necessary, while Skyas tried to shake off his disorientation but stumbled back to the ground like a toddler. When I get back up, you're fuckin' dead... he thought hazily, trying to clear his head.

The knights continued their assault, the one standing over Skyas raising his sword to stab downward. "And now they play the fools," he snarled. "Stand and fight, cowards!" The knight facing Cassian whirled to avoid his swing, attempting a vicious headbutt, while the third charged past the current engagement toward Xyla, sword raised.

V'ika decided to play the innocent. "I hardly know anyone here... or even what you group of people are! Why not just explain yourselves? Yes? They're only attacking in self-defense..."

Fiora ran backward as her shields broke, continuing to bolster Cassian with a steady stream of aether while keeping her distance. Across the battlefield, Xyla's fury erupted as she faced her attacker. "MOTHER LOVING GOBLIN FUCKER," she glared. "We aren't even from this shit realm, we came through a portal to deal with the dickhead 'Crimson King.' I will let loose if you don't stop." She drew her bowstring tighter, a shimmering dark arrow materializing as she aimed at the exposed joint in his armor.

Cassian turned his back on the knight who had headbutted him, taking a cut to his left shoulder before dashing to Xyla's aid. "No, you don't!" he shouted, swinging his axe at her attacker. Fiora's concentration faltered as her protective barriers shattered against the onslaught. Seeing Cassian throwing himself between Xyla and danger, she retreated several steps, weaving patterns to channel aether toward him.

The barrier stopped most of the damage while Skyas regained his bearings. Gritting his teeth, he kicked the knight away to gain space, grabbing his blade and slicing through a portion of the knight's stomach. "Thanks for the blunt force trauma. Unfortunately, all you did was piss me off... Given how strong the Collector and other commanders were, one would think you foot soldiers would be smart enough to leave us to the higher-ups of the Red King."

Ren charged in alongside Skyas, swiftly slashing the knight across the stomach before taking position at his rear, leaving the enemy exposed from behind, while Rove shouted desperately from the sidelines. "THIS REALLY SEEMS TO BE A REALLY LARGE MISUNDERSTANDING!" He dug through his bag, producing a pointed shield and hurling it at Xyla's attacker, hoping to cause some damage.

The knights hesitated, ceasing their attacks momentarily. They exchanged glances but said nothing at first. Down the tracks, torchlight appeared, accompanied by the sound of many more footsteps—metal on metal—suggesting their skirmish had drawn attention from a nearby encampment, likely where the trains stopped.

The knight disarmed by the taciturn Viera and injured by Ren cursed, quickly retrieving his weapon. "We need to leave now. Here is not the place for discussion." His voice was urgent but controlled. "Follow us, or stay and fight, we care not which you choose." Without waiting for a response, the three knights broke away and jogged deeper into the woods, leaving the Vanguard to decide between facing the approaching force or following these strange knights into the uncharted darkness.

"You guys did great by the way, real good work," C'verna remarked sarcastically, finishing her snack before following the retreating trio without hesitation. The taciturn Viera glanced over their shoulder at the approaching commotion before following, moving stealthily in hopes of learning more about these strange figures.

"We should follow them," Fiora stated quietly, her fingers tightening around her staff as her eyes darted between the retreating knights and the approaching torchlight. No trust softened her expression—only cold pragmatism. "They might know something." She glanced at Cassian, already reaching into her pouch for medical supplies as she assessed the wounded.

Xyla frowned at the sound of approaching reinforcements and followed without further comment, while V'ika sighed a resigned "Oh great..." as he trailed after the knights, hoping for the best. The large group down the tracks continued to approach.

Rove looked around in confusion before running to retrieve his shield, somehow shoving it into his impossibly spacious bag before following with unexpected enthusiasm. 

Despite his desire to "absolutely fucking demolish" the guard who had knocked him down, Skyas reluctantly sheathed his blade. "I'd love to beat the fuck out of you, but given you're one of the few people who haven't wanted to kill us and are willing to talk, the others would be mad if I passed," he grumbled as he followed.

Ren cast one final look at the approaching force before hauling himself after the others, disappearing into the darkness of the forest behind the mysterious knights.
